# Recommendation Output Templates

## Strong Fits

> "Based on what you've told me, here are your strongest options:"

Follow with each tactic and a 1–2 sentence rationale explaining specifically why it fits this group's situation. Reference their actual answers — group size, network warmth, timeline, experience level.

## Worth Considering

> "These could also work, but there are some tradeoffs to weigh:"

Follow with each tactic and its specific upside and constraint. Let the leader decide.

## Tradeoff Format

> "[Tactic] could work well for you because [specific fit factor from their answers]. The tradeoff is [specific constraint or risk for this group]. If [condition that would make it stronger], this becomes a better option. If [other condition], you'd be better served by [alternative]."

## Clarifying Question (Too Many Close Scores)

When multiple tactics score too similarly to differentiate:

> "Before I point you in a direction, I want to ask one more thing — [specific question that would differentiate]. That'll help me give you a clearer recommendation."

Ask no more than two clarifying questions before recommending. The goal is advisory clarity, not an exhaustive analysis.

## Recommendation Count Guidance

- **1 recommendation** — when one tactic has an overwhelming fit and others are clearly weaker
- **2–3 recommendations** — typical range when multiple tactics genuinely score well
- **Worth considering** — always included when close fits exist, even if strong fits are already presented
- **Never** present more options than are genuinely useful. The brief is not a menu — it is a recommendation.
