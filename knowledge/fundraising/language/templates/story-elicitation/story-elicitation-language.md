# Story Elicitation Language

## Primary Question

> "Tell me about something your group has done that you're proud of."

Do not ask "do you have a story?" or "can you share a story about your cause?" — leaders frequently self-disqualify.

## Dismissal Recovery — General

> "Some of the most effective fundraising stories are not about crisis — they're about what a community does together, what they've built, or what would be lost without it. What's something your group has that you'd genuinely miss if it were gone?"

## Dismissal Recovery — Neighbourhood / Community Orgs

> "What is one thing your neighbourhood or group has or does that you would genuinely miss if it were gone?"

Use this variant when working with a neighbourhood association, community group, or local service organisation. It almost always unlocks the story.

## Reflect Back (After Story Emerges)

Paraphrase what you heard to show understanding. Keep it short — you're confirming, not retelling.

> "So if I've got this right — [1–2 sentence paraphrase of the story in your own words]. Is that the story you want at the center of your campaign?"

## Confirm and Lock

> "Got it. [Story summary]. That's a strong foundation for this campaign."

Once confirmed, the story is set. Use it to personalise the brief throughout — execution steps, CTA language, and messaging.

## Guided Questions (No-Story Fallback)

If both the primary question and dismissal recovery do not surface a story:

> "Let me try a different angle. Who is most directly affected by what your group does?"

> "Is there a specific person whose situation changed — even a little — because of your work?"

> "Has anything shifted in your community because your group exists?"

> "Was there a moment that made this personal for you, or for someone in your group?"

If a story emerges from any of these: return to the full elicitation flow (reflect back → confirm → lock).

If no story emerges after all four questions: flag the gap in the brief and make story development Step 1 of the execution plan.

## Gap Flag Language (No Story Found)

For the brief's Your Story section:

> "Your story is a work in progress. The most important next step before you launch is developing the personal story at the center of your ask. A story doesn't need to be dramatic — it needs to be specific and true. [Include the guided questions above as a starting point for the leader to work through independently.]"
