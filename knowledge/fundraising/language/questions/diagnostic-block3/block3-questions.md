# Diagnostic Block 3 — Capacity and Assets

These questions map what the leader actually has available — people, channels, and content capacity. Answers feed into the Your Team section of the brief and into Layer 2 fit scoring.

Block 3 splits on solo vs. group (established in framing).

---

## Group Path

**Q1 — Group size and active vs. sharers**
> "How many people are in your group altogether? And of those, how many would you say are active — willing to make asks or take on tasks — versus people who'd share content but not much more?"

**Q2 — Role mapping**
> "For the people who are more active — what kinds of things are they good at or willing to do? Think about things like: making direct asks, handling logistics, posting on social media, showing up to events, following up with donors."

*Note: For event-based tactics, role mapping is elevated to a minimum requirement — not optional.*

**Q3 — In-person reach**
> "Beyond your contact list, does your group have any regular in-person reach — places you see people, events you're part of, gatherings you attend?"

**Q4 — Events**
> "Does your group run any in-person events of your own — meetings, gatherings, community events?"

**Q5 — Social media presence**
> "Does your group have a social media presence? Which platforms, and how active would you say you are — regular posting and engagement, or more occasional?"

**Q6 — Content capacity**
> "Honestly — can your group sustain regular updates throughout the campaign? Things like posting, sending messages, following up. Or is your bandwidth more limited?"

If high capacity → content-intensive tactics viable.
If low capacity → simpler tactics; essentials-only execution plan.

*Note: Content capacity is a meaningful differentiator in close calls between otherwise similarly scored tactics.*

---

## Solo Path

**Q1 — Recruitable help**
> "Is there anyone you could bring in to help — even informally? Someone who could make a few calls, share some posts, or show up to help at an event?"

If yes:
> "Roughly how many people, and what would they be comfortable doing?"

If no:
> "No problem — we'll build this as a plan for one person."

**Q2 — In-person reach**
> "Beyond your contact list, do you have regular in-person access to people — events, meetings, gatherings where you could make an ask?"

**Q3 — In-person events**
> "Are there any in-person events or gatherings coming up where you could make a fundraising ask?"

**Q4 — Social media**
> "Do you have a personal social media presence you'd be comfortable using for this campaign? Which platforms, and how active are you?"

**Q5 — Content capacity**
> "Honestly — how much time and energy do you have to keep the campaign active? Things like posting, sending messages, following up. Just you, on your own."

If high capacity → content-intensive tactics viable for solo.
If low capacity → simpler tactics with essentials-only plan.
