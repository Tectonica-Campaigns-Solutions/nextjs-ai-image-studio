# Diagnostic Block 1 — Motivation and Context

These questions establish the foundational context for the entire campaign. Every answer feeds directly into the Campaign Brief's Fundraising Goal and Context section and into the Layer 2 fit scoring.

## Questions

**Q1 — Cause and purpose**
> "What are you fundraising for? Is it for a specific cause, campaign, or purpose?"

**Q2 — Org campaign or independent**
> "Is this part of a broader org fundraising campaign, or is your group running this independently?"

If part of org campaign:
> "Has the org already communicated with your audience about this campaign? This helps us understand whether your ask is a first touchpoint or a reinforcing one."

> "Does your org need to approve campaign materials before they go out — messaging, promotional content, event communications? If so, how quickly can they turn approvals around? This affects your timeline."

**Q3 — Cash donations**
> "Do you want to collect or accept cash donations as part of your fundraiser?"

If yes:
> "Does your org have a process in place for handling and tracking cash donations? If not, we'll need to sort that out before you proceed."

*Note: No process for cash handling → hard stop for all in-person giving tactics. Flag immediately.*

**Q4 — Target amount**
> "Do you have a target amount or donation range in mind?"

If yes:
> "Is that a total campaign goal, a per-donor ask, or both?"

If no:
> "Do you have a sense of the giving capacity of your audience — are you thinking small grassroots donations, mid-level gifts, or a mix?"

**Q5 — Deadline**
> "Is there urgency? Is there a deadline you're working toward?"

**Q6 — Lead time**
> "How much time do you have to plan and run this fundraiser?"

*Note: Lead time shapes tactic recommendation — a leader with two weeks has a very different set of viable options than one with three months. Do not conflate deadline with lead time.*

**Q7 — Recurring or one-time**
> "Is this a one-time effort or something you'd want to run again?"

**Q8 — Sustainability vs. growth**
> "Are you trying to sustain what your group already has, or to grow your reach or donor base?"

*Note: This is distinct from recurring vs. one-time. A group can want a one-time effort that sustains their existing programs. The brief tone must match the stated goal.*

**Q9 — Prior fundraising history**
> "Has your org fundraised from this audience before? Through what channels?"

*Note: Personal experience was captured in framing. This question is about org-level history — the two are distinct.*

**Q10 — Personal connection to the issue**
> "Are you or anyone in your group personally affected by the issue you're fundraising for?"

*Note: Feeds story potential in Block 4. If yes, flag as a strong story lead.*
