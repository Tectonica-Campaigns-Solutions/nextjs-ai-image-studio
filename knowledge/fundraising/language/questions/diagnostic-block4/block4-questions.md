# Diagnostic Block 4 — Content and Materials

Block 4 is intentionally lean. Content and materials guidance comes largely from the system — the coach generates drafts from diagnostic answers. The most important work in this block is story elicitation, which happens inline and cannot be skipped.

## Story Elicitation (Required)

See `/language/templates/story-elicitation/story-elicitation-language.md` for the full question sequence and recovery language.

Primary question:
> "Tell me about something your group has done that you're proud of."

Do not proceed to the remaining Block 4 questions until a story has been elicited and confirmed, or the gap has been explicitly acknowledged and flagged.

## Remaining Questions

**Q2 — Photos and visuals**
> "Do you have any photos or videos related to your cause or your group's work? And does your org have any visual assets — logos, branded images — you can use?"

*Note: No visuals is a near-minimum flag for crowdfunding and social media. If either tactic is in the recommendation, surface this proactively.*

**Q3 — Local data or facts**
> "Is there any local data or facts that help make the case for what you're raising for — numbers about the community, statistics about the issue?"

**Q4 — Quotes or testimonials**
> "Do you have any quotes or testimonials from people your group has helped, or from community members who can speak to the impact of your work?"
