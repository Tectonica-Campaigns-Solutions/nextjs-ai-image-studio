# Diagnostic Block 2 — Audience and Relationships

These questions map the leader's audience segments and how they reach them. Answers feed directly into the Your Audience section of the brief and into Layer 2 fit scoring.

## Questions

**Q1 — Who are you asking?**
> "Who are you planning to ask for donations? It's fine if it's a mix — I just want to understand the different groups."

Four categories to identify and probe:
- People the leader has personally recruited or is in close contact with
- The org's existing contact list or database
- Cold or new contacts (people who don't know the leader or the org yet)
- A combination of the above

For each segment identified, follow up:

> "Roughly how many people are in that group?"
> "Have you been in contact with them recently?"
> "Have any of them donated before — to you or to the org?"
> "How warm is the relationship — do they know you well, or is it more of an acquaintance?"
> "What kind of donation amount feels realistic for this group?"

*Note: Prior giving history is a strong conversion predictor. Flag prior donors as high-priority segment.*

**Q2 — How do you interact with each segment?**
> "How do you typically reach or interact with each of these groups — in person, digitally, or a mix?"

If in-person:
> "What settings do you see them in? Events, meetings, regular gatherings?"

If digital:
> "Which channels do you use most — email, social media, messaging apps?"

If mix:
> "Which feels most natural for a fundraising ask?"

**Q3 — Org communication approach**
> "How does your org typically communicate with and fundraise from these audiences? Is there an established channel or approach?"

**Q4 — Matching gift**
> "Do you have access to any kind of matching gift — from a donor, a business, or anyone else?"

Three paths:
- Confirmed matching gift → route to Matching Gift Campaign Sub-A
- Working on securing one → route to Matching Gift Campaign Sub-B
- No match → no action on this question; continue

**Q5 — Existing giving mechanism**
> "Does your group already have any way for people to give — a membership fee, a donation jar, a QR code, anything like that?"

*Note: If yes and the leader conflates membership fees with donations, flag as a missed revenue opportunity — formalising what already exists is easier than building from scratch.*

**Q6 — Contact list organisation**
> "Is your contact list all in one place, or is it spread across different lists, spreadsheets, or apps?"

*Note: Multiple fragmented lists → flag as data quality risk. Note in brief's Guardrails and Reminders section.*

**Q7 — Follow-up plan**
> "After the campaign, how are you planning to follow up with people — donors and non-donors? And do you have the bandwidth to do that?"
