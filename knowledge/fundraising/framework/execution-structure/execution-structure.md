# Execution Steps Structure

Execution steps in the brief are self-contained action plans — not coaching scripts. They are designed to be followed by the leader independently, without the coach present.

## Six Properties of Execution Steps

1. **Sequenced** — numbered and ordered by what needs to happen first
2. **Tailored to solo vs. group** — different steps where the approach diverges
3. **Calibrated to experience level** — first-timers receive more explanation and context; experienced leaders receive a faster, more direct plan
4. **Time-anchored where relevant** — steps reference relative timing (Week 1, Day of event, 48 hours before launch) rather than fixed dates
5. **Connected to the Graphic Helper** — a contextual handoff prompt appears at the step where imagery is most useful; a second appears at the end of the brief
6. **Inline deliverable drafts** — all scripts, email copy, social posts, thank you messages, and follow-up messages are generated as actual draft text. No placeholders.

## Experience Flag Behaviour

The experience flag (set during framing) shapes both the depth of explanation in each step and the overall pace:

- **Experienced leaders** — faster, more direct plan; minimal context-setting
- **First-timers** — more explanation per step, more check-ins built into the plan, specific context for why each step matters
- **Somewhat experienced** — intermediate calibration; assume interpersonal confidence, less assumption on digital or formal fundraising process

## Four Sub-Sections

### Your Team (group tactics only)

Role map drawn from Block 3 role mapping answers. Lists each active helper by name or role with assigned responsibilities. Makes the plan an accountability tool the org can review and approve.

Solo tactics skip this section and go directly to Your Plan.

### Your Content Calendar

Suggested posting and outreach schedule for the full campaign window:

- **Time-limited campaigns** — day-by-day or week-by-week schedule from launch to close
- **Evergreen campaigns** — periodic activation schedule with trigger moments and suggested cadence
- **Event-based tactics** — two parallel calendars: event logistics milestones and fundraising/promotion milestones

The content calendar is generated as a standalone section so it can be shared directly with team members.

### Your Plan

Phased, numbered steps with inline deliverable drafts generated from diagnostic answers and org templates. Phase names vary by tactic (Prepare / Launch / Sustain / Follow up is the default pattern).

### Follow-up Plan

Always the final step regardless of tactic. Covers:
- Thank you messages (donors and non-donor attendees where applicable)
- Impact reporting
- Donor retention and next steps
- Results update to the broader community
