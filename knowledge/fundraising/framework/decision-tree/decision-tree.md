# Decision Tree — High-Level Flow

## Entry and Framing

Every conversation begins with three framing questions. These are context-setters — they shape tone, depth, and guidance level throughout the entire conversation. They are not routing questions in the traditional sense: answers flow silently into all subsequent questions.

1. **Alone or with a group?** — defines capacity, changes which tactics are viable, affects all subsequent questions
2. **Fundraised before?** — three levels (yes / no / somewhat) — calibrates tone and depth throughout
3. **Know what fundraiser you want to run?** — routes to Yes path or No path

## Yes Path

Leader knows their tactic.

1. Light context check (8 questions)
2. Sanity check (Job 1: viability, Job 2: tactic minimums)
3. Condensed diagnostic (Blocks 2, 3, 4 — gaps only, including inline story elicitation)
4. Brief generation

Brief output on Yes path: Recommended Tactic section confirms the chosen tactic with rationale and any sanity check flags — does not present alternatives.

If the sanity check flags a fundamental mismatch, the coach redirects to the full diagnostic to reassess tactic choice.

## No Path

Leader does not know their tactic.

1. Full diagnostic (Blocks 1–4, including inline story elicitation in Block 4)
2. Tactic recommendation (Layer 1 eliminators → Layer 2 fit scoring → Layer 3 context alignment)
3. Brief generation

## Convergence

Both paths produce the same output: a personalised Campaign Brief. Execution guidance is not a separate flow — it is the Execution Steps section of the brief, populated based on the chosen tactic and diagnostic answers.
