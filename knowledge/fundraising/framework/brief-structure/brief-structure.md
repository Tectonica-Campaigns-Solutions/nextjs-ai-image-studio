# Campaign Brief Structure

The Campaign Brief is the primary output of the Fundraising Coach. Every coaching conversation exists to gather what is needed to populate this document. The brief is generated on both the Yes path and the No path.

The brief is self-contained — designed to be followed by the leader without the coach present.

## Seven Sections

| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| Fundraising Goal and Context | What the leader is raising for, target amount, deadline, first-time vs. experienced |
| Recommended Tactic(s) | Yes path: confirmation of chosen tactic with rationale and any sanity check flags. No path: 2–3 recommended tactics with rationale based on group size, capacity, and audience. |
| Your Audience | Who they are asking, how they reach them, estimated giving capacity per segment |
| Your Story | The personal or human story at the center of the ask — elicited and confirmed inline during Block 4. If no story exists, the gap is flagged and story development is Step 1 of execution. |
| Execution Steps | Step-by-step action guide for the chosen tactic, tailored to solo vs. group and calibrated to experience level. Includes: Your Team (group only), Your Content Calendar, Your Plan, Follow-up Plan. |
| CTA and Key Messaging | Suggested ask language, subject lines, or scripts drawn from org brand templates and personalised to audience warmth, ask amount, and the confirmed story. |
| Guardrails and Reminders | Compliance flags for the chosen tactic, email collection reminder, follow-up plan, employer match note in thank you communications only. |

## Execution Steps Structure

Every brief includes four sub-sections within Execution Steps:

- **Your Team** (group tactics only) — role map drawn from Block 3 answers. Lists each active helper by name or role with assigned responsibilities. Solo tactics skip this section.
- **Your Content Calendar** — posting and outreach schedule for the full campaign window. Time-limited: day-by-day or week-by-week. Evergreen: periodic activation schedule. Event-based: two parallel calendars (logistics + fundraising).
- **Your Plan** — phased, numbered steps with inline deliverable drafts generated from diagnostic answers.
- **Follow-up Plan** — always the final step. Covers thank yous, impact reporting, and donor retention.

## Deliverable Generation Rule

Every script, email, social post, thank you message, and follow-up message in the brief is generated as actual personalised draft text. No placeholders. The leader reviews, adjusts to their voice, and uses.

## Graphic Helper Connection

A contextual handoff prompt appears at the step in the execution plan where imagery is most useful. A second prompt appears at the end of the brief: "Would you like to create campaign imagery? Connect to the Graphic Helper →"
