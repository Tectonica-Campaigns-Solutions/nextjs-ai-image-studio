# Peer-to-Peer Fundraising — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive guidance on how to support participants and why seed donors matter. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Meaningfully different. Solo path is a personal fundraising campaign — the leader is the sole solicitor. Group path adds participant coordination, individual targets per solicitor, and team momentum management.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Campaign lead** — owns the overall plan, coordinates participants, tracks progress
- **Fundraising participants** — each runs their own personal fundraising ask
- **Amplifiers** — share content and support participants but don't make direct asks
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages thank yous and tracks results

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm fundraising page setup**

Confirm that each participant has — or can set up — a personal fundraising page linked to your org's donation infrastructure. If individual peer-to-peer pages aren't supported by the org's platform, agree on a fallback before proceeding.

**Step 2: Confirm approval process for participant content**

Decide upfront: what can participants customise (their personal story, photos, tone), what must stay consistent (ask amount, donation page link, org framing), and whether customised content needs review before going live. Confirm who reviews and how quickly.

**Step 3: Develop your seed donor list**

Before anything goes public, identify your first 5–10 donors — people who will give in the first 48–72 hours. Early momentum is everything. A page with zero donations looks abandoned; a page with five donations signals something worth joining.

[Generate seed donor identification prompt: "Think about the 5–10 people closest to this cause — family, close friends, colleagues, or community members who would give quickly if you asked directly. These are your Day 1 asks before the page goes wide."]

**Step 4: Write your story and personal ask**

[Generate personal ask from org templates. Personalised to: confirmed story, ask amount, cause. Written in first person for easy personalisation. Includes clear CTA and donation page link.]

[If with a group, generate participant template with [YOUR NAME] and [YOUR PERSONAL CONNECTION TO THIS CAUSE] placeholders, and guidance on what can and cannot be changed.]

**Step 4b: Set individual targets per solicitor (group only)**

Each active solicitor needs their own individual target — not just a share of the overall goal. Individual targets create accountability. Targets should be calibrated to each solicitor's network size and warmth.

[Generate per-solicitor target table: solicitor name/role, primary network type, estimated network size, suggested individual target, segment-calibrated ask amount. Totals should meet or exceed the campaign goal.]

**Step 5: Prepare participant briefing (group only)**

[Generate participant briefing: what we're doing and why, how to set up and personalise your page, your ask template, what you can and cannot change, seed donor instructions, content cadence guidance, approval process, and who to contact with questions. Written for a non-professional audience.]

---

## Phase 2 — Launch

**Step 6: Activate seed donors first**

Before the page goes public, personally reach out to each seed donor — direct personal message only, not a broadcast.

[Generate short personal seed donor message. Personalised to: story, ask amount. Tone: warm and direct.]

**Step 7: Confirm participants are ready (group only)**

Before going public, confirm each participant has their content ready and — if approval is required — cleared. Set a clear submission deadline if review is needed. Do not launch until all participant content is confirmed.

**Step 8: Launch publicly**

Once you have at least 3–5 donations on the page, share publicly across all channels.

[Generate launch messages for each confirmed channel — social media post, WhatsApp broadcast, email announcement. Each personalised to: story, current donation total, ask amount.]

[If with a group, coordinate so participants launch within the same 24-hour window.]

## Phase 3 — Sustain

📌 **Peer-to-peer campaigns require ongoing content and activity.** The longer the campaign and the larger the goal, the more participants need to post updates, make additional asks, and share personal moments. Campaigns that go quiet after launch stall. Brief participants on this expectation upfront.

**Step 9: Mid-campaign update (halfway through)**

[Generate mid-campaign update for each confirmed channel — celebrates progress, shares a moment from the story, encourages a final push. References current total vs. goal. Group participant versions included.]

**Step 10: Final push (48 hours before deadline)**

[Generate final push — short, urgent, deadline-focused. Generated only if deadline confirmed in diagnostic.]

---

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 11: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you messages for relevant channels. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 12: Results update to all participants and supporters**

[Generate results update — total raised, celebration of participant achievements, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA if campaign still open.]

**Step 13: Debrief with your team (group only)**

Within a week of closing, bring the team together — what worked, what didn't, who your strongest fundraisers were, what you'd do differently. This builds long-term fundraising capability.

**Step 14: Record results**

Note total raised, participants, donors, average gift size, and top individual fundraisers. Share with your org.

---

📌 **The seed donor step is the single most important step in peer-to-peer fundraising.** If a leader launches cold without seeded donations, the campaign is very likely to stall. Surface this clearly in the brief.

📌 **Treat the participant briefing like an influencer brief** — authentic personal voices within an approved framework. The goal is not uniformity; it is consistency on the non-negotiables (ask amount, link, org framing) with genuine personal expression everywhere else.
