# Peer-to-Peer Fundraising — Questions

Peer-to-peer fundraising works when group members or the leader personally solicits their own networks — not just shares a link. This tactic requires active solicitors, not just amplifiers.

## Minimum Requirement Checks

> "Do you have a personal network you can reach out to and ask to donate — or group members willing to do the same?"

> "Do you have a story — personal, from your group, or from someone your org has helped — that explains why this cause matters?"

> "Do you have a way to capture donor email addresses at the point of giving?"

**If with a group:**
> "Can you confirm that at least some of your group members are willing to actively ask people to donate — not just share a link?"

**If solo:**
> "Do you have at least 20 people in your personal network you could reach out to? If not, let's talk about whether this is the right tactic or how we'd expand your reach first."

## Optimization Questions

> "Do you have contact details for your network — email, phone, or messaging apps like WhatsApp?"

> "Are you active on social media? Which platforms do you use most?"

> "Is there a specific donation amount you want to suggest to people?"

> "Do you have a plan to get the first few donations in quickly before sharing more broadly? Early momentum matters a lot for this tactic."

> "For each person actively soliciting — what role are they best suited for? For example: personal ask lead, social media amplifier, follow-up coordinator. We'll use this to assign responsibilities in your plan."

> "How are you planning to follow up with donors after they give — a thank you, an update on impact?"
