# Gala / Dinner Fundraiser — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive full rationale and logistics guidance. Experienced leaders receive streamlined project plan structure.

**Group only.** A gala cannot be run by one person.

**Scale sub-variants shape complexity and timeline:**
- Small community dinner (20–50 people) → 3–4 weeks minimum
- Mid-size fundraising dinner (50–150 people) → 6–8 weeks minimum, org support needed
- Large formal gala (150+) → 8–12 weeks minimum, org staff involvement required

---

## Your Team

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Event chair** — overall ownership, final decision-maker, primary org liaison
- **Venue and logistics lead** — venue, catering, setup, permits, day-of operations
- **Ticketing lead** — ticket sales platform, guest list, registrations, seating
- **Sponsorship lead** — identifies and approaches potential sponsors
- **Programme and entertainment lead** — runs of show, speakers, in-room ask moment, entertainment
- **Fundraising and donations lead** — in-room ask, donation mechanisms, cash handling, silent auction if applicable
- **Promotions lead** — all pre-event communications
- **Follow-up coordinator** — thank yous, results, donor stewardship after the event

---

## Phase 1 — Foundation

**Step 1: Confirm scale, date, and venue**

Lock these three things before anything else. Nothing can be planned, promoted, or budgeted without a confirmed date and venue.

**Step 2: Check permits and licences**

Check what permits and licences are required — venue permit, liquor licence if alcohol is being served, any local authority requirements. Do not promote until resolved.

**Step 3: Confirm org support and approvals**

For mid-size and large events, confirm org staff or resources are available. Confirm what approvals are needed — for materials, messaging, ticketing, sponsorships, and public communications. Build all approval timelines into the project plan now.

**Step 4: Set up ticketing**

Confirm your ticketing mechanism — an online platform for ticket sales and registrations. Set your ticket price and confirm how ticket revenue reaches your org.

**Step 9: Plan the in-room fundraising moment**

A gala without a structured fundraising moment is a dinner party. Plan now: who makes the ask, when in the programme, the ask amount, how people give (QR codes, pledge cards, text to donate, or all three), and what happens with cash donations.

[Generate in-room ask script. Personalised to: confirmed story, ask amount, how to give, matching gift if applicable. Structure: personal story opening → emotional peak → specific ask with amount → what their gift will do → three ways to give right now. Short and powerful — under 3 minutes.]

## Phase 4 — Event Day

**Step 13: Set up donation materials visibly**

Before guests arrive, set up all donation QR codes, pledge cards, and collection points. Every seat should have a clear path to giving.

**Step 14: Execute the programme and fundraising ask**

Follow the programme. Make the in-room ask at the planned moment — typically after dinner, during dessert or coffee, when guests are comfortable and the room is warm. Use the prepared script.

**Step 15: Silent auction close and cash handling (if applicable)**

Close the silent auction at the announced time. Collect all cash and card donations according to your org's process. Do not leave the venue with unrecorded cash.

**Step 16: Thank donors from the stage**

A public acknowledgment during the event — "we've already raised X tonight, thank you" — creates momentum and social proof. Do this before the evening ends.

---

## Phase 5 — Close the Loop

**Step 17: Thank you messages to all attendees (within 24 hours)**

[Generate two thank you versions: (1) for donors — confirms gift, what it will do, warm and personal; (2) for non-donor attendees — thanks them for coming, soft follow-up ask. Optional employer match one-liner for donors.]

**Step 18: Follow up with sponsors (within 48 hours)**

[Generate sponsor thank you — total raised, event highlights, specific recognition for their contribution, next steps for any promised deliverables.]

**Step 19: Results update to full list**

[Generate results update — total raised, event highlights, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA for those who couldn't attend or didn't give.]

**Step 20: Debrief with your committee**

Within one week of the event, bring the full committee together — what worked, what didn't, venue and catering feedback, strongest supporters, and whether to make this an annual event.

**Step 21: Record results**

Note total raised, ticket revenue, donation revenue, sponsorship revenue, silent auction revenue if applicable, attendance, cash vs. digital split, and cost-to-raise ratio. Share full results with your org.

---

📌 **The project plan (Step 5) is the standout deliverable for this tactic.** A week-by-week milestone plan generated from the confirmed team and timeline is the difference between a successful event and a chaotic one.

📌 **The in-room ask script is as important here as at a house party** — it's the highest-stakes moment of the event and must be prepared, not improvised.

📌 **The cost-to-raise ratio matters specifically for galas** — they are the most expensive tactic to run. The org should understand the net return, not just the gross.

📌 **Sponsors who feel properly acknowledged are significantly more likely to sponsor again.** The sponsor thank you step is not optional.
