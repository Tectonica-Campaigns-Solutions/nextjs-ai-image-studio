# Phone / Call Banking — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive detailed script guidance including objection handling. Experienced leaders receive a streamlined script.

**Solo vs. group:** Meaningfully different. Solo path is the leader calling their own list personally. Group path coordinates multiple callers, divides the list, and tracks progress centrally.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Call coordinator** — manages the caller list, assigns contacts, tracks results
- **Callers** — each assigned a segment of the contact list
- **Follow-up coordinator** — sends thank you messages and tracks donations received

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm your contact list**

Confirm you have phone numbers for the contacts you plan to call. If some contacts only have email addresses, flag them — they'll need a different outreach channel.

[If with a group: divide the list among callers now. Assign contacts based on existing relationships where possible — people are more likely to give when they know the person calling.]

**Step 2: Confirm payment methods**

Before making any calls, confirm exactly how donors can give — and make sure every caller knows the answer. A donor who says yes on the call but can't figure out how to pay is a missed gift. Confirm: the donation page URL, whether the org accepts cheques or cash, and any alternative payment methods. Fallback for callers who get a payment question: "Let me find out and send you the details right now" — then follow up immediately.

**Step 3: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether your script or any materials need approval before use. Build this into your timeline.

**Step 4: Prepare your script**

[Generate call script. Personalised to: confirmed story, ask amount, audience warmth, deadline if applicable.
Structure: warm greeting and personal connection → brief story → specific ask with amount → what their gift will do → donation page link → how to handle a yes / how to handle "not right now" / how to handle a no.
First-timer version includes objection handling section with 3–4 common responses fully scripted.]

**Step 5: Prepare your tracking sheet**

[Generate call tracking template: name, phone number, date called, outcome (gave / call back / not interested / no answer), payment method used, notes, follow-up needed.]

---

## Phase 2 — Call

**Step 6: Make your calls**

Early evenings on weekdays and late mornings on weekends tend to work best. Avoid calling during dinner hours, early mornings, or late at night.

[If with a group: run a calling session together if possible — callers working at the same time creates energy, allows for real-time support on tricky questions, and makes it easier to track progress collectively.]

**Step 7: Leave a voicemail for no-answers**

For contacts who don't pick up, leave a short friendly voicemail and follow up with a text or WhatsApp.

[Generate voicemail script — warm, brief, references the cause and asks them to call back or check their messages.]
[Generate follow-up text/WhatsApp message — short, with donation page link.]

---

## Phase 3 — Follow Up

**Step 8: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you message. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 9: Follow up with "not right nows" (3–5 days later)**

For contacts who expressed interest but didn't give, a short follow-up message significantly improves conversion.

[Generate short follow-up — references the call, light touch, one-line ask with donation page link.]

**Step 10: Results update to callers and supporters**

[Generate results update — total raised, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA if campaign still open.]

**Step 11: Record results**

Note total calls made, contact rate, donation rate, total raised, and payment methods used. Share with your org.

---

📌 **Objection handling is especially important for first-timers.** Many non-professional callers freeze when someone says "not right now" or asks a payment question. Make sure every caller reads the objection handling section before their first call.

📌 **Group calling sessions make a meaningful difference to follow-through.** Calling alone is isolating. A shared session — even virtual — is strongly recommended for group campaigns.

📌 **Confirming payment methods before calling** is the most commonly missed prep step. A "yes" on a call that doesn't convert to a donation because the caller couldn't answer "how do I give?" is a preventable loss.
