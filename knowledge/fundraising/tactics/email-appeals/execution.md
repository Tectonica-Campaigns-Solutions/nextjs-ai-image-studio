# Email Fundraising Appeals — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive guidance on what makes a good fundraising email and compliance basics. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Largely the same. With a group, each member may send to their own list. Group path includes Your Team section.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Email lead** — owns the send, manages the list, tracks results
- **Email signer** — the name the email comes from. Establish this upfront: if the signer doesn't normally email this list, confirm their availability and any approval requirements before committing to a send date.
- **Content reviewer** — reviews and approves draft before sending
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages replies and tracks donors

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm your list and sending method**

Confirm who you're sending to and how. If sending to more than ~50 people, use an email platform rather than personal email to protect deliverability and ensure unsubscribe compliance.

**Step 2: Confirm legal compliance**

Before sending, confirm you understand the email marketing rules for your region — CAN-SPAM (US), GDPR (Europe), CASL (Canada). Core requirement: recipients must be able to unsubscribe. An email platform handles this automatically.

**Step 3: Confirm approvals and schedule**

Confirm two things before planning your send date: (1) who is signing the email and whether they've confirmed availability; (2) whether any content needs approval from your org or campaign lead. Build all approval lead time in before committing to a date.

**Step 4: Write your subject line and preheader**

Your subject line determines whether the email gets opened. Your preheader is your second chance.

---

## Phase 2 — Send

**Step 6: Send your campaign**

If you have a mix of warm and cold contacts, consider sending to your warmest contacts first — a day or two before the main list. Frame it as an exclusive early send: "I wanted to share this with you first before we go wider." This generates early donations that build credibility for the main send.

Tuesday through Thursday, mid-morning or early afternoon tend to perform best.

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 10: Campaign results email to full list**

Send a results update to your entire list — donors and non-donors. Thanks donors, shows non-donors the impact they missed, and keeps them warm for future asks. If the campaign is still open, include a soft invitation to still give.

[Generate results update email. Personalised to: total raised, number of donors, story callback, what the funds will do. Soft invitation for non-donors if campaign still open. Signed by email signer.]

**Step 11: Record results**

Note your open rate, click rate, number of donors, and total raised. These numbers help improve future campaigns.

---

📌 **The email signer is a planning dependency** — if the signer is someone other than the group leader, their availability must be confirmed before any send date is set. This is the most commonly missed planning step in email campaigns.

📌 **The warm-first send strategy** (Step 6) generates early donations that create credibility for the broader send. This is a meaningful performance difference, not just a nice-to-have.
