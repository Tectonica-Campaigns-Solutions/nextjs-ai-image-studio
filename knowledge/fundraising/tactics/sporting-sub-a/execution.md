# Sporting Events Sub-variant A — External Event Participation — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive rationale on how personal narrative drives donations. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Meaningful difference. Solo is one person fundraising around their own challenge entry. Group coordinates multiple participants each fundraising individually — essentially peer-to-peer structured around a shared external event.

📌 This tactic follows the same core structure as Peer-to-Peer with three key differences: (1) the event is externally organised — logistics are handled by the event organiser; (2) the personal challenge narrative is the primary fundraising driver; (3) there may be a minimum fundraising requirement that shapes goal and urgency.

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm your event entry**

Confirm entry is registered and locked. If there's a minimum fundraising requirement tied to entry, note it — this is your floor goal, not your ceiling.

**Step 2: Confirm your fundraising page setup**

Confirm each participant has a personal fundraising page linked to your org's donation infrastructure. Follow the same setup process as Peer-to-Peer Step 1.

**Step 3: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether any materials or messaging need approval before going out. Build this into your timeline.

**Step 4: Develop your personal narrative**

Challenge-based fundraising lives or dies on the personal story — not just the cause, but why you specifically are doing this challenge. What is the connection between the challenge and the cause? What does it cost you, and why is it worth it?

[Generate personal narrative development prompt: "Your donors aren't just supporting the cause — they're supporting you taking on something hard for it. Why this challenge? Why now? What does it mean personally?" Then draft a personal narrative from the confirmed story and challenge details.]

**Step 5: Develop your seed donor list**

Same as Peer-to-Peer — identify your first 5–10 donors before the page goes live. For challenge-based fundraising, these are often people who know you personally and will give based on their relationship with you.

[Generate seed donor identification prompt.]

**Step 6: Prepare your personal ask and launch content**

[Generate challenge-based personal ask. Personalised to: event name, challenge type, personal narrative, cause, ask amount, minimum fundraising requirement if applicable, donation page link. Includes: personal ask message, social media launch post, email announcement with subject line and preheader. Tone: personal, honest, energetic.]

[If with a group: generate participant template with [YOUR NAME], [YOUR CHALLENGE], and [YOUR PERSONAL REASON FOR DOING THIS] placeholders. Include what can and cannot be changed and approval process.]

## Phase 2 — Launch

**Step 7: Activate seed donors**

Personally reach out to seed donors before the page goes public — direct personal message only.

[Generate short personal seed donor message. Personalised to: challenge narrative, ask amount. Tone: warm and direct.]

**Step 8: Confirm group participants are ready (group only)**

Confirm all participant content is ready and approved before launch.

**Step 9: Go public**

Launch across all confirmed channels once seed donations are in. If with a group, launch within the same 24-hour window.

---

## Phase 3 — Sustain

**Step 10: Post regular training and challenge updates**

This is where challenge-based fundraising diverges most from standard peer-to-peer. Training updates, behind-the-scenes moments, setbacks, and milestones are powerful content — they keep donors emotionally invested in the journey and create natural moments to re-ask. The more personal and honest the updates, the better they convert.

[Generate content schedule based on time between launch and event — training update posts, milestone moments, final week push, event day post, and results. Draft content for each. For group participants: versions with [YOUR TRAINING UPDATE] placeholder.]

A post from the event — at the start, finish line, or both — is one of the highest-engagement moments of the campaign.

---

📌 **The personal narrative is the key differentiator from standard peer-to-peer.** The challenge story is distinct from the cause story — both need to be present. Why this challenge, why this person, why now.

📌 **Training updates as fundraising content** is a genuinely different approach. Donors love following a journey — personal, honest updates about the challenge itself are high-converting content.

📌 **The event day post is a unique high-engagement moment.** Don't skip it — it's often the most-liked and most-shared content of the entire campaign.
