# Raffles Sub-variant A — Raffle

Legal compliance is a hard stop — do not proceed until confirmed. No prize = no raffle.

## Entry Question

> "Are you planning a raffle — where participants buy tickets for a chance to win a prize — or a sweepstakes — where entry is free and donation is optional?"

## Minimum Requirement Checks

> "Have you checked the raffle laws in your area? Raffles are one of the most regulated fundraising activities — rules vary significantly by country, state, and municipality, and running one without the right licence can create serious legal problems. Has your org given you guidance on this?"

> "Do you have a prize confirmed? There's no raffle without a prize — what have you got, or how are you planning to source one?"

> "How are you planning to sell tickets — in person, online, or both?"

> "Are you planning to accept cash for ticket sales? If so, does your org have a process for handling and tracking cash?"

> "Do you have a way to capture email addresses from ticket buyers so we can notify the winner and follow up?"

## Optimization Questions

> "Have you thought about ticket pricing? We can help you work out a price that makes sense based on the prize value and your fundraising target."

> "Do you have contact details for people you want to promote the raffle to — email, phone, or messaging apps?"

> "If fundraising with a group, how many people can help sell tickets?"

> "Are you active on social media? We can help you promote the raffle."

> "How are you planning to draw the winner and announce the result?"
