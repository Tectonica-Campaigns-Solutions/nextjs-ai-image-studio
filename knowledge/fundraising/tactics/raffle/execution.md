# Raffles Sub-variant A — Raffle — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive full legal guidance and ticket mechanics. Experienced leaders receive direct action items — but the legal compliance gate applies regardless of experience level.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Raffle coordinator** — owns the plan, manages legal compliance, holds the written process
- **Ticket sales team** — each sells tickets across their own networks
- **Cash handling lead** — manages all cash ticket sales and tracks receipts
- **Prize coordinator** — manages the prize, draw, and winner notification
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages thank yous and results

---

## Phase 1 — Legal and Logistics (before anything else)

**Step 1: Confirm legal compliance**

This is a hard gate — do not proceed until resolved. Confirm with your org whether you have the required licence or permit to run a raffle in your area. If your org doesn't have guidance on this, flag it to them now before building any further plan.

[Surface the legal compliance flag and prompt confirmation before proceeding.]

**Step 2: Confirm your prize**

[Generate prize sourcing suggestions if needed, based on org type and community — local business donation, org-provided prize, event experience, or service. Personalised to confirmed cause and community context.]

**Step 3: Confirm your ticket sales mechanism**

Confirm how tickets will be sold — in person, online, or both. A standard org donation page is not sufficient for ticket sales — a dedicated mechanism is required. If selling in person, confirm how cash sales will be handled and tracked.

**Step 4: Set your ticket price**

[Generate ticket pricing guidance: minimum price to make the goal viable, suggested price range based on prize value, note on the sweet spot between affordability and margin.]

**Step 5: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether your raffle promotion, ticket design, or any public communications need approval before going out.

**Step 6: Prepare ticket and promotional materials**

[Generate raffle promotional content for each confirmed channel. Personalised to: prize description, ticket price, draw date, cause, story, and how to buy. Includes social media posts, email with subject line and preheader, WhatsApp message.]
[Generate ticket template: raffle name, prize, draw date, price, and terms.]

> **→ Create raffle promotional imagery or ticket design: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 7: Confirm group member templates (group only)**

[Generate group member ticket sales template with [YOUR NAME] placeholder and guidance on what can and cannot be changed.]

---

## Phase 2 — Sell Tickets

**Step 8: Launch ticket sales**

Open ticket sales across all confirmed channels. If running group ticket sales, coordinate so all sellers launch within the same window.

**Step 9: Mid-campaign push (halfway through ticket sales window)**

[Generate mid-campaign push — tickets sold so far or remaining, prize reminder, draw date countdown, purchase link.]

**Step 10: Final push (48–72 hours before draw)**

[Generate final push — urgency, prize reminder, draw date, how to buy last-minute tickets.]

---

## Phase 3 — Draw and Close the Loop

**Step 11: Conduct the draw**

Conduct the draw according to your org's process and any legal requirements for your jurisdiction. Document the process.

**Step 12: Notify and thank the winner**

[Generate winner notification message — warm, congratulatory, prize confirmation, delivery instructions.]

**Step 13: Announce the winner publicly (with permission)**

A public winner announcement — with the winner's permission — builds credibility and excitement for future raffles.

[Generate public winner announcement — celebratory, thanks participants, announces winner by first name or however they've agreed to be identified.]

**Step 14: Thank you to all participants (within 24 hours of draw)**

[Generate thank you message to everyone who bought a ticket — thanks them for supporting the cause, what the funds will do, soft invitation to stay involved.]

**Step 15: Results update to full list**

[Generate results update — total raised, winner announcement if public, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA for non-participants.]

**Step 16: Record results**

Note total raised, tickets sold, cash vs. online split, which channels drove the most sales, and whether to run the raffle again. Share with your org.

> **→ Create imagery for raffle promotion or future campaigns: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

---

📌 **The legal compliance gate applies regardless of experience level.** It is always Step 1 and nothing proceeds without it.

📌 **The winner announcement is a named step** — it builds credibility and social proof for future campaigns. Always confirm the winner's permission before any public announcement.
