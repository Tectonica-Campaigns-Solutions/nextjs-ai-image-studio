# Crowdfunding — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive page setup guidance and detailed content advice. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Meaningful difference. Solo is one person running their own campaign. Group coordinates multiple people promoting a shared page across their own networks.

**Campaign type:** Time-limited (launch → promote → close) vs. Evergreen (ongoing page with periodic promotion). Steps adapt accordingly — evergreen notes are marked throughout.

📌 This plan uses a single shared campaign page. If individual participant pages were requested, this should have been redirected to Peer-to-Peer during the diagnostic.

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Set up your crowdfunding page**

Your page needs four things to convert: a compelling title, your story, a clear goal with a deadline, and strong visual content.

[Generate crowdfunding page copy. Personalised to: confirmed story, goal amount, deadline, cause. Includes: page title, opening paragraph, story section, impact statement, CTA. Note that visual content is essential and must be added before the page goes live.]

**For evergreen campaigns:** Set a cumulative fundraising goal rather than a deadline. Update the goal periodically as milestones are reached.

> **→ Create campaign imagery for your crowdfunding page: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 2: Confirm your goal and deadline**

For time-limited campaigns: confirm goal and deadline are set on the page. A visible countdown creates urgency. If you don't have a hard deadline, set one — open-ended campaigns significantly underperform.

**Step 3: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether page content needs approval from your org or campaign lead before going live. Build this into your timeline.

**Step 4: Develop your seed donor list**

Before the page goes live, identify your first 5–10 donors. This is the single most important preparation step — a page that launches with zero donations stalls quickly. A page with several early donations signals momentum to everyone who visits after.

[Generate seed donor identification prompt: "Think about the 5–10 people closest to this cause who would give quickly if asked directly. These are your Day 1 asks before the page goes public."]

**Step 5: Prepare your launch content**

[Generate launch content for each confirmed channel: social media post, email announcement with subject line and preheader, WhatsApp broadcast. Each personalised to: story, goal, deadline, page link.]

[If with a group: generate group member content template with [YOUR NAME] and [YOUR PERSONAL CONNECTION TO THIS CAUSE] placeholders. Include guidance on what can and cannot be changed, and a note on the approval process if applicable.]

## Phase 2 — Launch

**Step 6: Activate seed donors**

Before making the page public, personally reach out to each seed donor — direct personal message only, not a broadcast.

[Generate short personal seed donor message. Personalised to: story, ask amount. Tone: warm and direct.]

**Step 7: Confirm group members are ready (group only)**

Before going live, confirm each group member has their content ready and — if approval is required — cleared. Set a clear deadline for submissions. Do not launch until all content is confirmed.

**Step 8: Go live**

Once you have at least 3–5 donations on the page, publish and send your launch content across all channels simultaneously.

[If with a group: coordinate so all members share within the same 2–4 hour window. Concentrated early activity boosts platform visibility.]

## Phase 3 — Sustain

📌 **Crowdfunding campaigns require ongoing content to maintain momentum.** Going quiet after launch is one of the most common reasons campaigns stall. For evergreen campaigns, periodic promotion is what keeps the page alive.

**Step 9: Post regular updates throughout the campaign**

**For time-limited campaigns:**
[Generate content schedule based on campaign length. Example for 2-week campaign: Day 1 — launch. Day 3 — story update. Day 7 — halfway milestone. Day 10 — donor spotlight or impact moment. Day 13 — final push. Day 14 — results. Draft post included for each. Group member versions included.]

**For evergreen campaigns:**
[Generate reusable promotion template pack — story update template, milestone celebration template, giving occasion activation template, donor spotlight template. Each designed to be updated with current context before sending. Promotion calendar note: suggest 3–4 planned activation moments per year alongside organic triggers.]

**Step 10: Respond to donors publicly**

When people give, acknowledge them publicly on the campaign page and social channels where appropriate. Public recognition encourages others to give.

**Step 11: Final push (time-limited only — 48 hours before deadline)**

[Generate final push content — short, urgent, deadline-focused, references current total and gap to goal.]

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 12: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you messages for relevant channels. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant. For evergreen: becomes a standard triggered message sent to each new donor.]

**Step 13: Results update or periodic campaign update**

**For time-limited:**
[Generate results update — total raised, story callback, celebration, gratitude. Soft CTA if still open. Pointer to how donors can stay connected — ongoing relationship, not a completed transaction.]

**For evergreen:**
[Generate periodic campaign update — cumulative total raised, story callback, what funds have done, invitation to give or share. Tone: ongoing momentum, not closure.]

**Step 14: Record results**

Time-limited: total raised, donors, average gift, conversion rate, channels that drove most traffic.
Evergreen: cumulative total, donors per period, average gift trend, which promotional moments drove the most giving.
