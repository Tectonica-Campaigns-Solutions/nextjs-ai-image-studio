# Tribute / Memorial Giving Sub-variant B — Occasion / Seasonal — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive rationale on why occasion-based giving works and how to express authentic community connection. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

📌 **For extended occasions** — Ramadan, Diwali season, the Christmas/holiday giving period — the campaign window spans weeks. The plan accounts for this with sustained content across the full window and natural peak moments within it (e.g. the opening of Ramadan, the last 10 nights, Eid al-Fitr).

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Campaign lead** — owns the plan, coordinates timing, manages org relationship
- **Content lead** — creates and manages content across the campaign window
- **Community outreach team** — each reaches their own community networks personally
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages thank yous and tracks results

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm the occasion, exact dates, and campaign window**

Confirm the specific occasion, its start and end dates, and any key moments within it. Calculate your lead time before the occasion begins — everything is calibrated to this.

- Under 1 week before start: personal outreach and social sharing only
- 1–2 weeks before start: add email and coordinate group outreach for a sustained window campaign
- 2+ weeks before start: full plan including content series across the full occasion window

**Step 2: Confirm the org's campaign frame and assets**

Confirm your org has provided messaging, campaign assets, or a campaign frame for this occasion. Your group activates around the org's framing. If assets aren't ready, flag to your org contact immediately.

**Step 3: Check for a matching gift**

Is there a matching gift running alongside this occasion — from the org, a group-recruited donor, or a contact the leader secured? If yes, lead every piece of content with the match. Extended occasions like Ramadan are strong moments to approach potential match donors.

**Step 4: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether campaign communications or additional content needs approval before going out. For extended campaigns, also confirm whether content needs approval on a rolling basis throughout the window.

**Step 5: Create your campaign content plan**

Your group shares a genuine personal connection to this occasion — that authenticity is your strongest asset.

[Generate content schedule based on the specific occasion and campaign window. For single-day or short occasions: launch post, mid-campaign update, final push, results. For extended occasions: opening of the occasion, sustained mid-window content series (story moments, community updates, donor spotlights), peak moment push (e.g. last 10 nights of Ramadan, Christmas Eve), and closing results. Draft post for each moment across relevant channels. Match leads every piece if confirmed. Tone calibrated to the specific occasion — solemn for remembrance, joyful for celebration, solidarity-focused for awareness periods.]

## Phase 2 — Launch and Sustain

**Step 7: Send personal outreach at campaign opening**

Personal messages to warm contacts at the start of the occasion significantly outperform broadcast-only approaches — especially where the group shares a personal cultural or religious connection to the moment.

[Generate personal outreach message — warm, personal, references the occasion and the group's authentic connection to it, cause link, donation page link. Match details included prominently if applicable.]

**Step 8: Post your opening content**

Post across all confirmed channels. If coordinating with a group, launch within the same window to create concentrated momentum at the opening of the occasion.

**Step 9: Sustain content across the occasion window**

For extended occasions, follow your content schedule throughout the window.

[Generate draft posts for each scheduled update — story moments, community updates, progress reports, peak moment pushes. Each personalised to the occasion rhythm and current campaign context. Group member versions included.]

**Step 10: Peak moment push**

At the natural peak moment of the occasion, send your strongest, most personal push. This is the highest-converting moment of the campaign window.

[Generate peak moment push — emotionally resonant, occasion-anchored, personal, specific ask, match deadline if applicable.]

**Step 11: Final push (last day or closing of the occasion)**

[Generate final push — warm close to the occasion, gratitude, final CTA, match deadline if applicable.]

## Phase 3 — Close the Loop

**Step 12: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours of campaign close)**

[Generate thank you message. Personalised to: story, occasion, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 13: Results update to full list**

Anchor the gratitude in the occasion and the community that came together around it. Include a soft invitation for non-donors to still give or get involved. If a match donor agreed to public recognition, include their thank you here.

[Generate results update — total raised, occasion callback, community gratitude, story. Named match donor thank you if agreed. Soft CTA for non-donors. Tone matches the occasion's closing spirit.]

**Step 14: Record results**

Note total raised, which channels drove the most donations, how personal outreach compared to social broadcasting, which content moments generated the most response, and which occasions resonate most strongly with your community for future planning. Share with your org.

📌 **For extended occasions, the peak moment push (Step 10) is typically the highest-converting content piece.** The opening and closing matter but the peak — last 10 nights of Ramadan, Christmas Eve, the final day of a giving season — is where most giving happens.

📌 **The group's authentic personal connection to the occasion is the core asset of this tactic.** Encourage group members to express this in their own voice rather than forwarding generic content.
