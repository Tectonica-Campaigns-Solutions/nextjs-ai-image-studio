# Matching Gift Campaign Sub-variant A — Match Already Secured

The match may come from the org's campaign, a donor the group recruited, or a contact the group leader secured independently. All are valid and treated equally.

## Minimum Requirement Checks

> "Is the match commitment confirmed in writing? A verbal commitment that falls through mid-campaign is a serious credibility problem — let's make sure it's locked before you start promoting."

> "Are the match details confirmed — the total amount available, the deadline for donations to qualify, and any conditions?"

> "Do you have a way to capture donor email addresses at the point of giving?"

## Optimization Questions

> "Do you have a story or personal connection to the cause we can build the campaign around?"

> "Do you have contact details for your audience — email, phone, or messaging apps?"

> "Are you active on social media? A match is a powerful message that travels well on social."

> "Is there a specific donation amount you want to suggest?"

> "Would the match donor be comfortable being named publicly? A named match is more compelling than an anonymous one."

> "How are you planning to follow up with donors after the campaign?"
