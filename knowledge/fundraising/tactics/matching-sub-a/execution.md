# Matching Gift Campaign Sub-variant A — Match Already Secured — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive rationale on why matches work and how to communicate them effectively. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Note:** Matching gift campaigns are almost always layered onto another campaign rather than run standalone. The match is the message; the underlying tactic provides the mechanism. This execution plan covers the match layer — it should sit on top of the primary tactic's execution plan.

**Note:** The match may come from the org's campaign, a donor the group recruited, or a contact the group leader secured independently. All are treated equally.

## Phase 1 — Confirm and Prepare

**Step 1: Lock down the match details**

Before any promotion begins, confirm in writing: total match amount available, the deadline for donations to qualify, any conditions (minimum gift size, first-time donors only, etc.), and who the match donor is.

**Step 2: Decide whether to name the match donor publicly**

A named match donor is more compelling than an anonymous one. Check with the match donor whether they're comfortable being named publicly. Also confirm whether they're comfortable being thanked publicly in results communications.

**Step 3: Confirm the match mechanics**

Confirm how the match will be activated and paid — does the match donor give directly to the org after the campaign, or is there another process? Make sure this is clear before you promote — donors may ask.

**Step 4: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether your campaign messaging needs approval from your org or the match donor before going out. Build this into your timeline.

## Phase 3 — Close the Loop

**Step 10: Confirm the match donation with the match donor**

After the campaign closes, follow up with the match donor to confirm the total matched amount and when it will be transferred to your org. Set a specific date — don't leave this open-ended.

**Step 11: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you message — confirms their gift was matched, total raised, what the funds will do. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 12: Results update and public thank you to match donor**

[Generate results update — total raised including match amount, story callback, gratitude. If match donor agreed to public recognition: includes named thank you with a sentence on their contribution. Soft CTA for non-donors to still give. Tone: celebratory and warm.]

**Step 13: Record results**

Note total raised, match amount activated, which channels drove the most donations, and whether the match donor would consider doing this again. Share with your org.

📌 **Match mechanics confirmation is frequently overlooked but donors often ask how the match actually works.** A leader who can't answer clearly undermines confidence in the campaign.

📌 **The public thank you to the match donor in the results update is the most natural and impactful placement** — it connects their generosity directly to the results achieved and shows the community that real people back this cause.
