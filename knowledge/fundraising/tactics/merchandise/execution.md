# Merchandise / Product Sales — Execution Plan (Tier 2)

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive full rationale, pricing guidance, and fulfilment planning. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Campaign type:** Time-limited (launch → promote → close) vs. Evergreen (ongoing offering with periodic promotion). Steps adapt accordingly — evergreen notes are marked throughout.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Product lead** — owns product setup, pricing, and fulfilment plan
- **Content and design lead** — creates or coordinates product design and promotional materials
- **Sales and promotion team** — each promotes across their own channels
- **Fulfilment coordinator** — handles shipping, delivery, or in-person distribution
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages buyer thank yous and tracks results

---

## Phase 1 — Product Setup

**Step 1: Confirm your product**

[Surface product type and design status from diagnostic:
- Design ready → proceed to Step 2.
- Design needed → "The Tectonica AI image creation helper can help you build merchandise graphics. Connect to it here to create your design, then come back to continue your plan."
- Publication content needed → "For publication content like copy or editorial text, I can help you draft that directly. Let's work through the content you need before moving to production planning."]

**Step 2: Confirm production or fulfilment setup**

[Confirm and explain based on product type:
- **Print-on-demand:** confirm platform is set up and design is uploaded and approved. Strongly recommended for evergreen campaigns — removes inventory management entirely.
- **Physical inventory:** confirm stock levels, storage, and how orders will be fulfilled. For evergreen: confirm restocking process.
- **Handmade or donated goods:** confirm quantity, condition, and collection plan. Note: not well-suited to evergreen unless production can be sustained.
- **Publication:** confirm print production timeline — cookbooks, calendars, and zines take weeks or months. Publications can work as evergreen if sufficient print run is available.]

**Step 3: Set your pricing and confirm the margin**

[Generate pricing guidance: production cost, suggested selling price based on comparable products, minimum price to make the fundraising goal viable, note on the margin threshold below which the campaign isn't worth running. For evergreen: also includes a note on sustainable pricing that doesn't require constant discounting.]

**Step 4: Confirm your sales mechanism**

[Confirm based on channel from diagnostic:
- Online: confirm sales platform. A standard org donation page is not sufficient.
- In person: confirm where, when, and who manages sales. Confirm cash handling process. For evergreen: confirm how in-person sales will be triggered.
- Both: confirm how orders are tracked across channels.]

**Step 5: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether product design, promotional materials, or any public communications need approval. For evergreen, also confirm whether new promotional content needs approval each time or whether a standing approval covers recurring promotions.

---

## Phase 2 — Promotion

**Step 6: Create your promotional content**

**For time-limited campaigns:**
[Generate product promotional content for each confirmed channel. Personalised to: product name, price, campaign window, cause, story, how to buy, delivery or collection details. Includes: social media posts, email announcement with subject line and preheader, WhatsApp broadcast.]

**For evergreen campaigns:**
[Generate reusable promotional template pack — story update template, milestone celebration template, giving occasion activation template. Each designed to be updated with current context before sending. Promotion cadence note: suggest once every 4–6 weeks as a baseline unless there's a specific trigger.]

> **→ Create product imagery for your promotional posts: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 7: Confirm group member templates and approval (group only)**

[Generate group member promotional template with [YOUR NAME] and [YOUR PERSONAL REASON FOR SUPPORTING THIS CAUSE] placeholders. Include guidance on what can and cannot be changed. For evergreen: confirm whether standing approval covers recurring group promotions.]

**Step 8: Launch promotion**

For time-limited: launch across all confirmed channels simultaneously.
For evergreen: launch with an initial promotion burst, then activate the template pack at regular intervals or triggered moments. Avoid over-promoting.

---

## Phase 3 — Sales and Fulfilment

**Step 9: Manage orders and fulfilment**

[Generate order tracking template: buyer name, contact details, product ordered, quantity, payment received, fulfilment status (pending / dispatched / collected), tracking reference if applicable. For evergreen: becomes a permanent operational tool.]

**Step 10: Mid-campaign push (time-limited only)**

[Generate mid-campaign push — items sold or remaining, story callback, purchase link. Scarcity message if stock is limited.]

**Step 11: Final push (time-limited only — last 48–72 hours)**

[Generate final push — urgency, last chance to order, deadline for delivery before any relevant date.]

---

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 12: Thank you messages to buyers (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you message — thanks buyers for their purchase and support, confirms what the funds will do. Warm and personal. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant. For evergreen: becomes a standard triggered message sent with each purchase confirmation.]

**Step 13: Results update or periodic store update**

**For time-limited:**
[Generate results update — total raised, products sold, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA for non-buyers if stock remains.]

**For evergreen:**
[Generate periodic store update — total raised to date, products available, story callback, store link. Light promotional tone. Quarterly or at natural moments like a milestone or product refresh.]

**Step 14: Store maintenance and refresh (evergreen only)**

[Generate evergreen store maintenance checklist: sales and revenue review, stock levels or POD health check, design refresh assessment, promotional calendar review for upcoming trigger moments, approval status check for any new products. Schedule every 3–6 months.]

**Step 15: Record results**

Time-limited: total raised, units sold, net margin (revenue minus production costs), which channels drove most sales, whether this product is worth running again.
Evergreen: cumulative total, units sold per period, net margin trend, which promotional moments drove the most giving.

📌 **The image creation helper integration is the standout differentiator for this tactic.** Surface it prominently at the design stage (Step 1) and the promotional imagery stage (Step 6) — these are the two moments where the tool handoff adds the most value.

📌 **Print-on-demand is strongly preferred for evergreen campaigns** — it removes inventory management entirely and scales automatically with demand.

📌 **The margin threshold note in Step 3 is important** — campaigns that sell items with near-zero margin waste significant effort for minimal return. This is flagged before the leader commits to a product, not after.
