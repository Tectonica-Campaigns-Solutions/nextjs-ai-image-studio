# Tribute / Memorial Giving Sub-variant A — Memorial — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive more context and gentler framing with check-ins at key decision points. Experienced leaders receive direct action items with the same care but lighter scaffolding.

📌 **This is the most emotionally sensitive tactic in the system.** The tone throughout should be noticeably warmer and slower than any other tactic. Do not apply fundraising pressure. Do not rush.

**Timing sub-variants shape the pace:**
- Recent passing → simplified, faster plan focused on essentials only
- Anniversary or remembrance → standard planning timeline
- Standalone tribute → most flexibility, full plan

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Campaign lead** — coordinates the campaign, holds the relationship with the family or community
- **Outreach team** — each reaches their own segment of the honoree's community
- **Content lead** — manages the tribute page and all communications
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages thank yous and tracks results

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm timing and approach**

[Surface timing confirmed in diagnostic and set expectations:
- Recent passing: "We'll keep this simple and focused. The most important things are getting the page live quickly with the right story and reaching out personally to the people who knew [name] most."
- Anniversary or remembrance: "You have time to plan this properly. We'll build a campaign that honours [name] well and reaches everyone who cared about them."
- Standalone tribute: "There's no time pressure. We'll build a thoughtful campaign at whatever pace works for you."]

**Step 2: Confirm family or community awareness**

Before any page goes live or any communication goes out, confirm that the family or next of kin — or relevant community leadership for a community or public figure — is aware of and comfortable with this fundraiser. For a recent passing, this is essential.

**Step 3: Confirm the story**

[Generate tribute narrative from Block 4 story. Who they were, what they meant to the community, and why fundraising in their name matters. Tone: warm, respectful, specific to the person. Not generic.]

**Step 4: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether the tribute page content, communications, or any public materials need approval — from your org, the family, or community leadership.

**Step 5: Set up the tribute page**

Set up the tribute page with the confirmed story, the honoree's name and photo if available and appropriate, the fundraising goal, and the donation page link. Review carefully before making it live.

> **→ Create tribute imagery or page graphics: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 6: Prepare your outreach list**

Segment your list: closest contacts first, then broader community. The most effective outreach reaches people who knew or cared about the honoree personally.

[Generate outreach framing: "Think about the people who knew [name] best — family, close friends, colleagues, and community members who would want to give in their memory. These are your first personal contacts."]

---

## Phase 2 — Reach Out

**Step 7: Send personal outreach to closest contacts first**

A personal message — not a broadcast — to the people closest to the honoree. Timing and tone matter here more than any other tactic.

[Generate personal outreach message for closest contacts. Tone calibrated to timing: gentle and brief for recent passing, fuller and warmer for anniversary or standalone tribute.]

**Step 8: Broader community outreach (after closest contacts)**

[Generate broader outreach content for each confirmed channel — respectful, honours the person, cause connection, donation page link. Avoids being transactional. Includes social media post, email with subject line and preheader, WhatsApp message.]

> **→ Create imagery for your tribute page or outreach posts: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 9: Confirm group member content and approval (group only)**

[Generate group member outreach template with [YOUR NAME] and [YOUR RELATIONSHIP TO OR MEMORY OF (name)] placeholders. Tone guidance for the specific timing and nature of the tribute. Confirm approval if required — consistency of tone is particularly important for memorial campaigns.]

---

## Phase 3 — Sustain (if campaign window is more than a few days)

**Step 10: Mid-campaign update (if appropriate)**

For anniversary or standalone tribute campaigns, a mid-campaign update keeps momentum going. For recent passings, skip this step unless it feels right — do not apply fundraising pressure to a community in grief.

[Generate mid-campaign update — focuses on the honoree's story and impact rather than fundraising pressure. References current total as a measure of community love, not urgency. Only generated for anniversary or standalone tribute timing.]

**Step 11: Final push (if deadline exists)**

[Generate gentle final push — warm, respectful, references the occasion, soft urgency. Avoids aggressive countdown language. Only generated if deadline confirmed.]

---

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 12: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you message — warm, personal, references the honoree, what the funds will do in their memory. Tone: grateful and reflective.]

**Step 13: Results update to full outreach list**

[Generate results update — total raised, tribute to the honoree, how the funds will be used, community gratitude. Soft CTA for non-donors. Tone: warm and closing rather than promotional.]

**Step 14: Share results with family or community**

Share the final results with the family or relevant community leadership directly — as a personal update, not a general broadcast.

[Generate short personal results message for family or community leadership — total raised, what it will go toward, gratitude for their support of the fundraiser.]

**Step 15: Record results**

Note total raised, donors, average gift size, and which outreach channels were most effective. Share with your org.

> **→ Create tribute imagery for your page or outreach: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

---

📌 **The mid-campaign update is a conditional judgement call.** For recent passings, the default should be to skip it unless the campaign lead feels it is appropriate. The brief surfaces this decision explicitly rather than assuming.

📌 **The family or community update as a named closing step** is important — it closes the loop with the people who matter most and builds trust for future fundraising.
