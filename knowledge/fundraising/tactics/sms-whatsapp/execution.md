# SMS / Text / WhatsApp Fundraising — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive consent and tone guidance. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Largely the same. With a group, multiple senders each reaching their own contacts multiplies reach significantly. Group path includes Your Team section.

**Send method note:** Steps adapt based on send method confirmed in diagnostic — personal one-to-one, WhatsApp broadcast, or SMS platform.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Message lead** — coordinates the send, manages timing, tracks responses
- **Senders** — each sends to their own personal contacts or assigned list segment
- **Follow-up coordinator** — tracks who responded, manages replies, follows up with non-responders

📌 **WhatsApp broadcast caveat for all senders:** Messages only deliver to contacts who have your number saved. If senders are unsure, suggest a pre-send message: "Hey, wanted to make sure you have my contact saved — I'm sharing something important soon."

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm your send method**

[Confirm and explain send method from diagnostic:
- **Personal one-to-one:** highest conversion, most time-intensive. Best for warm lists up to ~30 contacts per sender.
- **WhatsApp broadcast:** reaches multiple people at once but only delivers to contacts who have your number saved.
- **SMS platform:** best for larger lists. Confirm platform access and opt-in status before proceeding. Standard SMS is 160 characters — messages over that split, which can look unprofessional.]

**Step 2: Confirm opt-in status**

Confirm that everyone on your list has opted in to receive messages. This is both a legal requirement and a relationship protection. Do not proceed until confirmed.

**Step 3: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether your message needs approval from your org or campaign lead. Build approval time into your schedule before committing to a send date.

**Step 4: Write your message**

[Generate draft message. Personalised to: confirmed story, ask amount, send method, donation page link.
- Personal/WhatsApp: short, conversational, formatted for readability — short paragraphs, no large blocks of text.
- SMS platform: character-count aware, under 160 characters where possible.]

[If sending with a group, generate personalised sender template with [YOUR NAME] and [YOUR CONNECTION TO THE CAUSE] placeholders.]

---

## Phase 2 — Send

**Step 5: Send your messages**

Early evening on a weekday or mid-morning on a weekend tend to perform best. Avoid early mornings, late nights, and Mondays.

If using WhatsApp broadcast: send a test message to yourself first to confirm formatting.

If sending with a group: coordinate so all messages go out within the same window. A cluster of personal messages on the same day creates organic momentum.

---

## Phase 3 — Follow Up

**Step 6: Personal follow-up to non-responders (2–3 days later)**

For warm personal contacts who haven't responded, a short follow-up significantly improves conversion.

[Generate short follow-up message — lighter tone, references the first message, one-line ask. For personal and broadcast sends only — not for SMS platform sends.]

**Step 7: Thank you to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate short thank you message. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Appropriate length for messaging channel. Optional employer match addition if relevant: "If your employer offers charitable donation matching, your gift could go even further — worth checking."]

**Step 8: Results update to full list**

Once the campaign closes, send a brief results update — thanks donors, shows non-donors the impact, and keeps them warm for future asks.

[Generate short results update. Total raised, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA if campaign still open.]

**Step 9: Record results**

Note how many messages were sent, response rate, number of donors, and total raised.
