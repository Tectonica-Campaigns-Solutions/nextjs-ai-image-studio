# Online Giving Days — Execution Plan (Tier 2)

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive rationale on how to amplify effectively. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Same core steps. Group multiplies reach through coordinated amplification across multiple personal networks.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Amplification lead** — coordinates timing and channel coverage across the group
- **Social media team** — each amplifies across their own channels on a coordinated schedule
- **Personal outreach lead** — manages direct personal asks to warm contacts
- **Follow-up coordinator** — tracks responses and manages thank yous

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm the org's campaign assets**

Before doing anything else, confirm you have everything from the org — donation page link, graphics, messaging, and any specific ask language. Do not start promoting until these are confirmed. If assets aren't ready, flag to your org contact immediately — giving days have fixed deadlines.

**Step 2: Confirm the exact date and your lead time**

Confirm the giving day date and calculate how much time you have. Your plan scales to your lead time:
- Under 48 hours: personal outreach and social sharing only
- 3–7 days: add an email send and coordinate group outreach
- 1–2 weeks or more: full plan including warm list personal outreach, content series, and group coordination

**Step 3: Check for a matching gift**

Is there a matching gift — from the org's campaign, a group-recruited donor, or a contact the leader secured independently? If yes, this is your single most powerful message — every piece of content should lead with the match. Confirm: total amount, deadline, any conditions. If no match yet, consider whether there's someone in your community who might commit to one before the giving day.

**Step 4: Decide what additional content you are creating**

The org has provided campaign assets — but you may want to create additional content to make your promotion feel personal. Decide: are you personalising the org's materials with your own story and voice, or creating additional original content? If creating original content, check whether it needs org review before going out.

**Step 5: Confirm approval needs**

Confirm whether any content you create beyond the org's materials needs approval. For group members, also confirm whether their customised content needs review.

**Step 6: Prepare your personal outreach list**

Giving days are won on personal asks, not social broadcasting. Before you start posting, identify the people in your network most likely to give — your warm list.

[Generate personal outreach framing: "Think about the people in your network who care about this cause and have given or engaged before. These are your Day 1 personal asks before you go wide on social. A personal message to 20 warm contacts outperforms 200 social posts for a giving day."]

**Step 7: Prepare your content**

[Generate content for each confirmed channel, built around the org's campaign assets and personalised to: confirmed story, giving day name and date, match details if applicable (org or group-sourced), donation page link. Includes: social media posts (launch, mid-day push, final push), personal outreach message, email with subject line and preheader if time allows. Match leads every piece of content if confirmed.]

## Phase 2 — Launch and Amplify

**Step 9: Send personal outreach first**

On the morning of the giving day, personally reach out to everyone on your warm list before posting on social. Direct messages convert significantly better than social posts for giving days.

[Generate personal outreach message — short, warm, personal, references the giving day and why it matters to you. Match details included prominently if applicable.]

**Step 10: Post your launch content**

Post across all confirmed channels. If coordinating with a group, launch within the same 1–2 hour window to create concentrated momentum.

**Step 11: Post your mid-day update**

A mid-day update with a running total keeps momentum going and creates urgency.

[Generate mid-day update — current total, story callback, urgency note, donation page link. Match reminder if applicable.]

**Step 12: Post your final push (1–2 hours before deadline)**

[Generate final push post — short, urgent, countdown to deadline, current total vs. goal. Match deadline called out prominently if applicable.]

---

## Phase 3 — Close the Loop

**Step 13: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you message. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 14: Results update to full list**

Send a results update to your full list — donors and non-donors alike. Thank donors publicly, share the total raised, and include a soft invitation for anyone who didn't give to still get involved — giving days often have a window after the headline date.

[Generate results update — total raised, story callback, gratitude. Explicit soft CTA for non-donors to still give if window is open, or to follow the org's work if closed.]

**Step 15: Record results**

Note total raised, which channels drove the most donations, and how personal outreach compared to social broadcasting. Share with your org.

---

📌 **Personal outreach first is the single most important giving day principle.** Social posts alone rarely move the needle for most group leaders. Personal messages to warm contacts before going wide on social is what drives early donations and momentum.

📌 **If a match exists — org or group-sourced — it must lead every piece of content.** Match messaging is the highest-converting giving day message available. It is never optional when a match is confirmed.
