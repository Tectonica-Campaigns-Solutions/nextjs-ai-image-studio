# Community Events — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive full logistics guidance and guidance on the fundraising moment within the event. Experienced leaders receive streamlined steps.

**Group only.** Solo leaders should have been redirected at sanity check.

📌 Art shows and benefit concerts follow the same core structure with nuances: art shows may involve artist agreements, sales commissions, and display logistics; benefit concerts involve performer agreements, ticketing, sound, and a more formal programme. For complex productions (multi-act concerts, large exhibitions), treat as closer to a gala in planning overhead and lead time.

---

## Your Team

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Event coordinator** — owns the overall plan, manages logistics, primary contact on the day
- **Activity leads** — each responsible for running a specific activity (bake sale table, car wash station, etc.)
- **Cash handling lead** — manages all cash and card transactions, tracks donations, captures email addresses from cash donors
- **Promotions lead** — handles all pre-event promotion
- **Materials coordinator** — prepares and brings all signage, QR codes, flyers, and donation materials
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages thank yous and tracks results

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare (2–4 weeks before event)

**Step 1: Confirm event format and venue**

Confirm exactly what activities you're running, where, and when. Everything else depends on this being locked.

**Step 2: Check permits and permissions**

Before any promotion begins, check whether you need permits or permissions — from your local council, venue, or relevant authority. Do not promote the event until this is resolved.

**Step 3: Confirm cash handling process**

Confirm with your org exactly how cash and card donations will be handled and tracked on the day. Confirm: who handles cash, how it is counted and recorded, where it goes after the event, and how donors receive receipts. Also confirm how email addresses will be captured from cash donors — needed for receipts and follow-up since cash transactions don't go through the donation page automatically.

**Step 4: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether promotional materials or event communications need approval from your org before going out. Build this into your timeline.

**Step 5: Prepare your event materials**

Every touchpoint at the event should make giving easy and visible. Prepare:
- Signage explaining the cause and how to donate
- QR code cards or flyers linking to the donation page
- A sign-up sheet for email addresses from cash donors
- Cash donation envelopes or a clearly labelled collection point if accepting cash

## Phase 2 — Final Preparations (2–3 days before)

**Step 8: Send reminder to your network**

A reminder 2–3 days before the event significantly boosts attendance.

[Generate reminder message for each confirmed channel — short, warm, event details, why it matters.]

**Step 9: Brief your team**

Make sure every team member knows their role, where to be, what to bring, and what to do if something goes wrong.

[Generate team briefing from Your Team role assignments: arrival time, setup responsibilities, cash handling procedures including email capture for cash donors, donation page link and QR codes, ask schedule, and who to contact on the day.]

## Phase 3 — Event Day

**Step 10: Set up donation materials visibly**

Before guests arrive, set up all donation signage, QR codes, and collection points. Giving should be visible and easy from the moment people arrive.

**Step 11: Make fundraising asks throughout the day**

Don't rely on passive giving. For longer events, plan multiple brief asks at natural energy moments — when a new group arrives, when an activity peaks, and again near the end.

[Generate brief, repeatable ask script — 60 seconds, story-led, specific amount, clear how to give (donation page and cash both referenced if applicable). Designed to be repeated by different team members at different points during the event.]

**Step 12: Thank donors in real time**

Publicly acknowledge giving during the event where appropriate — "we've already raised X today, thank you!" creates social momentum and encourages others to give.

**Step 13: Close cash handling properly**

At the end of the event, count and record all cash received according to your org's process before leaving the venue. Capture email addresses from cash donors.

📌 **The in-event direct ask is the most commonly skipped step at community events and the one that makes the biggest difference to total raised.** For longer events, multiple asks throughout the day significantly outperform a single ask moment or passive giving only.

📌 **Cash handling close is a named, explicit step.** This is where compliance problems most commonly occur — making it a named step keeps it front of mind for the team.
