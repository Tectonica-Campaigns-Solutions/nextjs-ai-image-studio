# Local Business Partnerships — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive guidance on the business ask conversation and relationship management. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Meaningful difference. Solo manages the partnership and promotion alone. Group distributes promotional responsibilities and can drive significantly more traffic.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Partnership lead** — manages the business relationship, holds the written agreement
- **Promotion lead** — coordinates the group's promotional effort
- **Social media team** — each promotes across their own channels
- **Event support** — shows up on the day and helps drive attendance
- **Follow-up coordinator** — tracks results and manages thank yous

---

## Phase 1 — Negotiate and Confirm

**Step 1: Approach the business (if not yet confirmed)**

[Generate business outreach script. Personalised to: org name, cause, story, proposed partnership type, what the group can offer in return (promotion, audience, social media reach). Tone: professional, warm, mutual benefit framing.]

**Step 2: Confirm the agreement in writing**

Before any promotion begins, confirm all terms in writing — the donation amount or percentage, the date or campaign window, what both sides will do to promote it. A simple email confirmation is sufficient if a formal contract isn't available.

[Generate written confirmation template for the leader to send to the business.]

**Step 3: Confirm mutual promotional review requirements**

Before creating any materials, confirm: (1) does the business need to review and approve anything that mentions their name or brand before it goes public? (2) does the group's org need to review materials that mention the business? Resolve both before designing anything.

**Step 4: Confirm the business's promotion commitment**

The partnership should be mutual. Confirm exactly what the business will do — social media posts, in-store signage, email to their customer list, or similar. If the business won't commit to any promotion, reconsider the partnership.

**Step 5: Confirm payment methods and procedures**

Confirm exactly how donations will reach your org — through the donation page, a percentage transferred after the event, or a direct donation. Make sure everyone on your team knows how this works before promoting.

**Step 6: Confirm org approval needs for all communications**

Check whether the partnership announcement or any public communications need approval from your org before going out. Build this into your timeline.

## Phase 2 — Create Materials and Promote

**Step 7: Create leave-behind materials for the venue**

Materials displayed at the business venue capture giving from people who discover the campaign through the business, not through your network — cards, flyers, or posters with your donation page QR code and a brief story.

> **→ Create leave-behind or event materials for the business: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 8: Create your promotional content**

[Generate promotional content for each confirmed channel. Personalised to: business name, partnership type, event date or campaign window, cause, story. Includes: social media posts, email announcement with subject line and preheader, WhatsApp message. Flag if business approval is required before posting.]

> **→ Create promotional imagery for this partnership: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 9: Confirm group member templates and approval (group only)**

[Generate group member promotional template with [YOUR NAME] and [YOUR PERSONAL CONNECTION TO THIS CAUSE] placeholders. Include guidance on what can and cannot be changed, and any business brand requirements to observe.]

**Step 10: Launch promotion**

Send promotional content across all channels once all approvals are confirmed. For a percentage-of-sales night, start promoting at least one week before the event.

[If with a group: coordinate so all members promote within the same window.]

---

## Phase 3 — Event or Campaign Day

**Step 11: Send a day-of reminder**

A morning-of reminder significantly boosts turnout.

[Generate day-of reminder for each confirmed channel — short, warm, includes time, location or link, and why it matters.]

**Step 12: Thank the business publicly**

Post a public thank you to the business during or immediately after the event. This recognises their contribution, encourages their customers to view them positively, and models the kind of partnership you want to continue.

[Generate public thank you post for relevant social channels.]

---

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 13: Confirm the donation amount and transfer**

Follow up with the business after the event to confirm the final donation amount and when it will be transferred to your org. Set a specific date — don't leave this open-ended.

**Step 14: Thank you messages to supporters**

[Generate thank you message to everyone who showed up, shared the promotion, or engaged. Personalised to: cause, what was raised. Soft CTA if there's a follow-on action.]

**Step 15: Results update**

[Generate results update — total raised, story callback, gratitude to the business and supporters.]

**Step 16: Record results**

Note total raised, estimated attendance or reach, which promotional channels drove the most response, and whether this partnership is worth repeating. Share with your org.

> **→ Create additional imagery for your results update or future partnership promotion: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

---

📌 **Confirming the donation transfer with a specific date** is the most commonly skipped step — and where donations most often get delayed or lost.

📌 **The business thank you post serves double duty** — good relationship management and good public PR for both the org and the business. It also increases the likelihood of a repeat partnership.
