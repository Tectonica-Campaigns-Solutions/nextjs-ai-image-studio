# Social Media Fundraisers — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive content guidance and platform-specific tips. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Meaningful difference. Solo is one person creating and posting content. Group coordinates multiple people posting across their own channels, which multiplies reach significantly.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Content lead** — creates core content, manages the posting schedule, distributes templates
- **Post scheduler** — ensures posts go out on time
- **Group amplifiers / posters** — each customises and shares content across their own channels
- **Content reviewer** — reviews group poster customisations before they go live if approval is required
- **Community manager** — monitors comments and replies, engages with donors publicly
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages donor thank yous and tracks results

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm your platform and approach**

[Confirm approach from diagnostic:
- **Platform-native tool** (Facebook Fundraiser, Instagram donation sticker): handles donation processing. Confirm org is registered as nonprofit on this platform before proceeding. If not, switch to campaign-to-page approach.
- **Campaign driving to donation page:** content links to org's donation page. Works on any platform, more control over messaging.
- **Group amplification:** group members each share content across their own channels. Requires content coordination and approval process.]

**Step 2: Confirm your content plan**

Social media fundraisers require a content series — not a single post. Plan posts across the full campaign window before you start.

[Generate content schedule based on campaign length. Example for 10-day campaign: Day 1 — launch with story and ask. Day 3 — behind-the-scenes or cause update. Day 5 — donor spotlight or impact moment. Day 7 — halfway progress update. Day 9 — final push. Day 10 — results. Draft post included for each.]

**Step 3: Prepare group poster templates and confirm approval (group only)**

If group members are posting on their own channels, treat this like an influencer brief — authentic personal voices within approved guardrails. Decide upfront:
- What can group members customise freely (personal connection to the cause, their own photos)?
- What must stay consistent (ask amount, donation page link, approved hashtags, org framing)?
- Does any customised content need review before going live? If yes — who reviews, how quickly, through what channel?

[Generate group poster template pack: core message and story in first-person, [YOUR PERSONAL CONNECTION TO THIS CAUSE] placeholder, approved ask language, donation page link, approved hashtags, clear guidance on what can and cannot be changed. Includes a one-paragraph brief for group posters.]

**Step 4: Confirm approval needs for all content**

Check whether core campaign content — and group poster templates — need approval from your org before going out. If group members need to submit customised posts for review, confirm turnaround time so it doesn't delay launch.

**Step 5: Create your launch content**

[Generate launch content for each confirmed platform. Personalised to: confirmed story, ask amount, goal, deadline, donation page link. Platform-specific formatting applied — shorter for X/Twitter, more personal for Facebook, visual-led for Instagram. Hashtag suggestions included.]

## Phase 2 — Launch

**Step 6: Distribute templates and confirm group posters are ready (group only)**

Send each group poster their template pack and brief. Confirm they understand what they can customise, what stays fixed, and whether they need to submit for review. Set a clear deadline for submissions. Do not launch until all posts are cleared.

**Step 7: Post your launch content**

[If coordinating with a group: all members post within the same 2–4 hour window — concentrated activity boosts platform visibility and creates organic momentum.]

**Step 8: Engage immediately after posting**

Reply to early comments, thank early donors publicly, and reshare the post within the first few hours if engagement is strong. Platform algorithms favour content that generates early engagement.

---

## Phase 3 — Sustain

**Step 9: Post your content series**

[Generate draft posts for each scheduled update from the content plan — story moments, progress updates, donor spotlights, final push. Each personalised to current campaign context and platform. Group poster versions included.]

**Step 10: Respond to all comments and questions**

A campaign that goes unanswered feels abandoned. Responding publicly also encourages prospective donors.

---

## Phase 4 — Close the Loop

**Step 11: Post your results**

[Generate results post — total raised, story callback, celebration, gratitude. Soft CTA if still open.]

**Step 12: Thank you messages to donors**

[Generate thank you messages for each channel. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 13: Record results**

Note total reach, engagement rate, number of donors, total raised, and which platform, post, or group poster drove the most donations. Share with your org.
