# Sporting Events Sub-variant B — Self-Organised Event — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive rationale on how the two tracks work. Experienced leaders receive direct action items with cross-references.

**Group only.**

📌 **Two parallel tracks:** (1) Event track — logistics, venue, permits, format, day-of management (follows Community Events structure); (2) Fundraising track — participant pages or shared page, personal asks, content cadence (follows Peer-to-Peer or Crowdfunding structure). The fundraising track must start before the event track is fully confirmed.

📌 **Fundraising mechanism decision:** The most important choice in this tactic is whether participants fundraise individually (peer-to-peer track) or through a single shared page (crowdfunding track). If individual pages: follow Peer-to-Peer execution for the fundraising track. If single page: follow Crowdfunding execution for the fundraising track.

---

## Your Team

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Event coordinator** — owns event logistics, venue, permits, format, day-of operations
- **Fundraising coordinator** — owns the fundraising mechanism, participant pages or shared page, campaign communications
- **Participant lead (if P2P)** — briefs participants, distributes templates
- **Cash handling lead** — manages on-the-day cash and card donations, captures emails from cash donors
- **Promotions lead** — all pre-event promotion
- **Follow-up coordinator** — thank yous and results after the event

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm event format and fundraising mechanism**

Confirm: what kind of sporting event or tournament, where, when, and which fundraising mechanism:
- Individual participant pages → fundraising track follows Peer-to-Peer execution
- Single shared campaign page → fundraising track follows Crowdfunding execution

Note: peer-to-peer tends to work better when participants each have distinct networks to mobilise. Crowdfunding works better when the group wants a single unified campaign presence.

**Step 2: Check permits and permissions**

Check venue permits, route permissions, and local authority requirements. Do not promote publicly until resolved — but fundraising outreach can begin before permits are confirmed.

**Step 3: Confirm cash handling process**

Confirm how on-the-day cash and card donations will be handled and tracked. Confirm email address capture for cash donors.

**Step 4: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether materials or communications need approval. Confirm whether rolling content needs approval or just launch materials.

**Step 5: Follow fundraising track setup steps**

[Route to appropriate fundraising track:
- If peer-to-peer → Follow Peer-to-Peer execution Steps 3–6 (seed donor lists, personal ask templates, participant briefing, approval process).
- If crowdfunding → Follow Crowdfunding execution Steps 1–5 (page setup, goal and deadline, approval, seed donor list, launch content).]

**Step 6: Create event promotional content**

[Generate event promotional content. Personalised to: event name, format, date, time, location, cause, story, how people can participate, donate, or sponsor. Includes: social media posts, email announcement, WhatsApp broadcast.]

> **→ Create event promotional imagery: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 7: Prepare event materials**

Signage, QR codes, donation materials, cash handling setup, and email capture for cash donors — same as Community Events Step 5.

> **→ Create event signage or leave-behind materials: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

## Phase 2 — Launch Fundraising (before event)

**Step 8: Follow fundraising track launch steps**

[Route to appropriate fundraising track:
- If peer-to-peer → Follow Peer-to-Peer Phase 2 (activate seed donors, confirm participants ready, launch publicly).
- If crowdfunding → Follow Crowdfunding Phase 2 (activate seed donors, confirm group members ready, go live).]

**Step 9: Launch event promotion simultaneously**

Launch event promotional content across all channels at the same time as the fundraising launch.

## Phase 3 — Sustain

**Step 10: Follow fundraising track sustain steps**

[Route to appropriate fundraising track:
- If peer-to-peer → Follow Peer-to-Peer Phase 3 (participant content cadence, group progress updates, final push).
- If crowdfunding → Follow Crowdfunding Phase 3 (regular updates, respond to donors publicly, final push).]

## Phase 4 — Event Day

**Step 11: Send day-of reminder**

[Generate day-of reminder — short, energetic, event details, donation page link for supporters who can't attend.]

**Step 12: Make fundraising asks on the day**

Have a designated person make a brief ask at the start and again near the end, especially for attendees who haven't given yet.

[Generate brief event-day ask script — story-led, specific amount, QR code and donation page link.]

**Step 13: Close cash handling properly**

Count and record all cash before leaving the venue. Capture email addresses from cash donors.

---

## Phase 5 — Close the Loop

**Step 14: Follow fundraising track close-the-loop steps**

[Route to appropriate track:
- If peer-to-peer → Follow Peer-to-Peer Phase 4 (thank yous, results update, debrief, record results).
- If crowdfunding → Follow Crowdfunding Phase 4 (thank yous, results update, record results).]

**Step 15: Debrief with your team**

Within a week of the event, bring the team together to debrief on both the event and the fundraising. What worked in each track and what would you do differently?

**Step 16: Record results**

Note total raised, event attendance, cash vs. digital split, fundraising mechanism performance, and which promotional channels drove the most response. Share with your org.
