# In-Person Ask — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive step rationale and suggested language. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Solo vs. group:** Largely the same steps. With a group, multiple people can make asks at different touchpoints simultaneously. Group path includes Your Team section.

---

## Your Team (group only)

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Ask lead(s)** — makes the in-person ask
- **Materials coordinator** — prepares and brings leave-behind materials
- **Follow-up coordinator** — sends thank yous and tracks donors

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

**Step 1: Confirm your ask moment**

Identify exactly when in the meeting or event you will make the ask — beginning, end, or a natural pause. A brief, well-timed ask outperforms a long one. Aim for 2–3 minutes maximum.

**Step 2: Prepare your script**

[Generate draft script personalised to: confirmed story, ask amount, audience warmth, org tone. Structure: hook with story → why this matters now → specific ask with amount → what their gift will do → how to give. First-timer version includes a note to practise out loud before the event.]

**Step 3: Prepare your leave-behind**

A simple card or flyer with your donation page as a QR code. People who don't give in the moment need something to act on later — without it, conversion drops significantly.

> **→ Create a leave-behind graphic: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

**Step 4: Confirm email collection**

Confirm you have a way to capture donor email addresses — through the donation page or a sign-up sheet. Required for receipts and follow-up.

---

## Phase 2 — Make the Ask

**Step 5: Lead with the story**

The first 30 seconds determine whether people are listening. Lead with the human story, not the organisation.

[Confirmed story condensed to 2–3 sentences for verbal delivery.]

**Step 6: Make the specific ask**

Name the amount. "We're asking everyone here to consider a gift of [ask amount]" outperforms "whatever you can give." A specific number anchors giving.

**Step 7: Hand out leave-behinds**

Give everyone a leave-behind as you finish — even people who give on the spot. It reinforces the gift and gives them something to share.

---

## Phase 3 — Follow Up

**Step 8: Send thank yous (within 48 hours)**

[Generate personalised thank you message. Tone: warm and direct. Personalised to: story, ask amount, org name, how funds will be used. Include one-line employer match addition if relevant to audience: "If your employer offers charitable donation matching, your gift could go even further — worth checking."]

**Step 9: Follow up with people who didn't give (3–5 days later)**

[Generate gentle follow-up message. Personalised to: story, ask amount, urgency or deadline if applicable.]

**Step 10: Record what worked**

Note how many people were in the room, how many gave, and the total raised. This data helps improve future asks and demonstrates your group's contribution to the org.

> **→ Create campaign imagery for your next ask: Connect to the Graphic Helper**

---

📌 **The ask timing (2–3 minutes) is specific and practical** — most leaders don't know this instinctively. A shorter, well-prepared ask outperforms a longer one every time.

📌 **The leave-behind is not optional** — it is the mechanism for everyone who doesn't give in the moment. Without it, the ask ends when the meeting ends.
