# Walk / Run / Ride-a-Thon (Self-Organised) — Execution Plan

**Experience flag:** First-timers receive rationale on how the two tracks work together and why fundraising must start before the event is fully confirmed. Experienced leaders receive direct action items.

**Group only.** Solo leaders should have been redirected to Sporting Sub-A.

---

## Your Team

Populated from Block 3 role mapping:
- **Event coordinator** — owns event logistics, venue, permits, route, day-of management
- **Fundraising coordinator** — owns the fundraising plan, participant pages, campaign communications
- **Participant lead** — briefs participants, distributes templates, manages questions
- **Cash handling lead** — manages any on-the-day cash or card donations, captures emails from cash donors
- **Promotions lead** — handles all pre-event promotion
- **Follow-up coordinator** — manages thank yous and results after the event

---

📌 **Two parallel tracks run simultaneously from the start: (1) Event track** — logistics, venue, permits, route, day-of management; **(2) Fundraising track** — participant pages, personal asks, content cadence, donor communications. The fundraising track starts first — participants can begin personal outreach even before all event logistics are confirmed.

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare (3–6 weeks before event)

**Step 1: Confirm event format and logistics**

Confirm: what kind of event, where, when, distance or format, and any entry requirements. Everything depends on this being locked.

**Step 2: Check permits and permissions**

Check whether you need route permits, venue permissions, or local authority sign-off. Do not promote publicly until resolved — but participants can begin fundraising outreach before permits are confirmed.

**Step 3: Confirm participant fundraising page setup**

Confirm each participant can set up a personal fundraising page linked to your org's infrastructure. If not supported, agree on a fallback (shared donation page) now.

**Step 4: Confirm cash handling process**

Confirm how on-the-day cash and card donations will be handled and tracked. Confirm how email addresses will be captured from cash donors for receipts and follow-up.

**Step 5: Confirm approval needs**

Check whether any materials or communications need org approval. For a longer planning window, also confirm whether rolling content needs approval or just launch materials.

**Step 6: Brief participants and distribute fundraising templates**

Participants need to start fundraising before the event — not on the day.

[Generate participant briefing: what we're doing and why, how to set up your personal fundraising page, your personal ask template, what you can and cannot customise, seed donor list instructions, content cadence guidance for the fundraising window, approval process, event logistics (date, time, location, what to bring), and who to contact with questions. Written for a non-professional audience.]

**Step 7: Confirm approval process for participant content**

Decide upfront whether participant content needs review before going live — who reviews, how quickly, through what channel.

**Step 8: Develop seed donor lists**

Every participant should identify their first 5–10 donors before their page goes live.

[Generate seed donor identification prompt for participants: "Think about the 5–10 people closest to this cause who would give quickly if asked directly. Reach out to them personally before you share your page publicly."]

**Step 9: Create event promotional content**

[Generate event promotional content. Personalised to: event name, date, time, location, cause, story, how people can participate or donate. Includes: social media posts, email announcement, WhatsApp broadcast.]

## Phase 2 — Launch Fundraising (2–4 weeks before event)

**Step 10: Participants activate seed donors**

Before any page goes public, each participant personally reaches out to their seed donor list — direct personal message only.

[Generate short personal seed donor message for participants with [YOUR NAME] placeholder.]

**Step 11: Confirm participant content is ready and approved**

Confirm each participant has their content ready and — if approval is required — cleared before their page goes public.

**Step 12: Participants launch their pages publicly**

Once seed donations are in, each participant shares their page publicly. Coordinate within the same 24–48 hour window to create collective momentum.

**Step 13: Event promotion launch**

Launch event promotional content across all channels simultaneously with the fundraising launch.

## Phase 4 — Event Day

**Step 16: Day-of reminder to your network**

A reminder the morning of the event boosts last-minute donations from people who want to support but can't attend.

[Generate day-of reminder — short, energetic, event details, donation page link for supporters who can't attend.]

**Step 17: Make fundraising asks on the day**

Have a designated person make a brief fundraising ask at the start of the event and again near the end — especially for attendees who aren't participants and may not have given yet.

[Generate brief event-day ask script — short, energetic, story-led, specific amount, QR code and donation page link.]

**Step 18: Close cash handling properly**

Count and record all cash received before leaving the venue. Capture email addresses from cash donors.

---

## Phase 5 — Close the Loop

**Step 19: Thank you messages to donors (within 24 hours)**

[Generate thank you messages. Personalised to: story, how funds will be used. Optional employer match one-liner if relevant.]

**Step 20: Results update to full participant and supporter list**

[Generate results update — total raised, top fundraisers celebrated if applicable, event highlights, story callback, gratitude. Soft CTA for non-donors.]

**Step 21: Debrief with your team**

Within a week of the event, bring the team together — what worked, who the strongest fundraisers were, what you'd do differently. This builds long-term group fundraising capability.

**Step 22: Record results**

Note total raised, participants, donors, average gift size, cash vs. digital split, event attendance, top fundraisers, and which promotional channels drove the most response. Share with your org.

---

📌 **The two parallel tracks framing is the most important concept for first-timers.** The fundraising track must start before the event track is fully confirmed — participants can begin personal outreach even while permits are still being sorted. Do not wait for event logistics to complete before starting fundraising.

📌 **The debrief is essential for building long-term group fundraising capability** — not just a post-mortem on this event.
