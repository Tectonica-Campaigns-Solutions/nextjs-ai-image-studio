# Undivided — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Belonging before belief ↔ Trust through small risks** — [A2.4] × [A4.3]: belonging is not declared, it is built risk by reciprocated risk; the small risks are the mechanism, belonging the felt result. (Entries: "Belonging before belief", "Trust accrues through small reciprocal risks")
- **Belonging before belief ↔ Relationship not transaction** — [A2.4] × [A3.3]: the gift-mode relationship is what produces belonging; a transactional group never gets there. (Entries: "Belonging before belief", "Relationship, not transaction")
- **Safe-not-comfortable ↔ Trust through small risks** — [A6.5] × [A4.3]: bounded safety is precisely what makes a small risk thinkable, so safety is the precondition for the trust-building loop. (Entries: "Safe doesn't mean comfortable", "Trust accrues through small reciprocal risks")
- **Experience over information ↔ Safe-not-comfortable** — [A6.6] × [A6.5]: people only submit to the uncomfortable direct experience that changes them when the frame is safe enough to risk it. (Entries: "People change through experience and relationship", "Safe doesn't mean comfortable")
- **Bridging across difference ↔ Composition and balance** — [A4.6] × [A5.1]: bridges across difference don't form on their own; balanced composition is how a leader designs for them. (Entries: "Bridging across difference", "Designing the group")
- **Relationships sustain costly work ↔ Bridging across difference** — [A4.1] × [A4.6]: the ties that hold people under cost are often the cross-difference ones, so bridging is what makes the group resilient, not just diverse. (Entries: "Relationships are what sustain people through costly work", "Bridging across difference")
- **Relationships sustain costly work ↔ Peer accountability & ritual** — [A10.5] × [A8.3]: the relationships sustain people because they generate accountability — not wanting to let the community down is what keeps you in under backlash. (Entries: "Relationships are what sustain people", "Peer accountability and ritual community")
- **The 1:1 ↔ Relationship not transaction** — [A4.2] × [A3.3]: the individual meeting is the relational (not transactional) ask made concrete — you sit down to understand the person, not to extract a yes. (Entries: "The one-to-one", "Relationship, not transaction")
- **Extrinsic→intrinsic ↔ Freedom over compliance** — [A3.1] × [A3.2]: people only internalize motivation for work they freely chose, so the freedom to opt out is the precondition for genuine ownership. (Entries: "From extrinsic to intrinsic", "Freedom to choose over compliance")
- **Freedom over compliance ↔ Crafting roles to strengths** — [A3.2] × [A5.4]: freedom plus a well-fitted role is how a reluctant member becomes an owner; the role gives the freedom somewhere to land. (Entries: "Freedom to choose over compliance", "Crafting roles to people's strengths")
- **The spark vs. the vehicle ↔ Composition and the launch** — [A6.4] × [A5.7]: the launch is the spark; the vehicle is the structure that keeps the launched energy from dissipating, so they are two halves of one design problem. (Entries: "The spark vs. the sustained vehicle", "Designing the group")
- **The spark vs. the vehicle ↔ Peer accountability & ritual** — [A2.5] × [A6.1]: the "vehicle" that converts a spark into durability is built largely from recurring ritual and structured meetings — the rituals are what the vehicle is made of. (Entries: "The spark vs. the sustained vehicle", "Peer accountability and ritual community")
- **Bringing your full self ↔ Safe-not-comfortable** — [A1.4] × [A6.5]: a leader's authentic risk-taking is what widens the band of what the group will safely risk; the leader goes first. (Entries: "Bringing your full self", "Safe doesn't mean comfortable")
- **Revolution by trial and error ↔ The spark vs. the vehicle** — [A1.6] × [A6.4]: because the path is non-linear, the vehicle's job is to hold people through the backslides — durability is what makes trial-and-error survivable. (Entries: "Revolution by trial and error", "The spark vs. the sustained vehicle")
- **The unequal cost ↔ Bridging across difference** — [A10.4] × [A4.6]: the disproportionate burden on minority members is the shadow side of bridging; you cannot build bridges responsibly without managing the uneven cost of building them. (Entries: "The unequal cost", "Bridging across difference")

## Excluded from this summary

- **The racial-justice / antiracism subject matter as content.** The book's entire substantive subject — racism, white evangelicalism, racial identity development, the theology and history of evangelical engagement with justice (Lausanne, the SBC, the "other evangelicals," Christian nationalism) — is treated as the *vehicle* and excluded as RAG content. Retained only as retell-ready illustration of group-leadership mechanics, never framed as "about race." This is the largest single exclusion and the reason for the sub-band size.
- **Program design and curriculum mechanics** — the six-week structure, the weekly themes (Redeem/Relationship/Reality/etc.), the specific exercises (string-web/intersectionality, laminated photo cards, racial-identity surveys, the repentance/forgiveness exercise), the on-ramps → **H:org-level** / understanding-not-execution gate; kept only at the why-level where a mechanism (designed composition, productive discomfort) transfers.
- **Crossroads as an institution and its internal politics** — the church's growth, finances, "Super Bowl of Preaching," the board/power dynamics, Brian's leadership of the church, the LGBTQIA+ controversy, the church's ambivalent relationship to Undivided → org-level, not a group-leader's call.
- **Scaling and sustainability of the program** — expansion to other churches, the Miami Township police adaptation, the business model, going online → **H:org-level**.
- **The four biographies as biography** — Jess's incarceration and recovery, Sandra's marriage, Grant's job in the prison system, Chuck's faith journey and sabbatical are mined only for retell-ready moments; their full personal narratives are cut.
- **Cincinnati political and electoral material** — Issue 44, the Tensing/DuBose trials, the Bethel protest, the Christian Coalition / Reed-Robertson electoral history → political-history context; the Reed-Robertson "ground game vs. air game" story is retained *only* as an illustration of mobilizing-vs-organizing, stripped of its partisan specifics.
- **Author's framing for a general/academic reader** — the personal frame (Han's own background), the methods discussion, and the social-movement-theory contribution are addressed to readers, not group leaders.

## Tagging notes & open questions (for the prune)

1. **The triple gate (the headline).** This source's defining feature is that its *subject* is not the coach's subject. Every Example field is written to be retold as a group-leadership illustration without invoking race; confirm in tag-testing that retrieval never surfaces this as a "race/DEI" source. If it does, the subject-reframe note needs to be louder or the entries need de-specifying further.
2. **Register-gap: "who carries the cost" (FLAG 1).** "The unequal cost" entry has no clean leaf — tagged A10.4 + A6.5, A4.6 inline. This is the strongest register-gap candidate from this source: the corpus has no home for *equity of burden / cost within a group across power differences*. Recommend considering a leaf (e.g., under A10 collective-care or A6 dynamics) if it recurs. Do **not** mint here.
3. **A4.6 as primary, not background (FLAG 2).** Bridging-across-difference is a load-bearing primary in this source, not a lens in the background. Two entries ("Bridging", "The unequal cost") lean on it. Confirm A4.6 carries the retrieval rather than collapsing into A4.1.
4. **Spark→vehicle spans three leaves (FLAG 3).** Tagged A6.4 + A2.5 + A5.7 across the "spark vs. vehicle" entry. This is the mobilizing-vs-organizing lens made concrete for a small group; watch whether it wants a tighter cross-link or resolves cleanly to A2.5 in testing.
5. **Han-source overlap / de-duplication.** Overlapping concepts are deliberately short and cross-referenced (relationships-are-strategy → *Prisms*; intrinsic motivation → Pink's *Drive*; structure-before-process and 3–7 size → Fisher's *Collective Edge*; bonding-vs-bridging → *Prisms*). Confirm the coach pulls the *depth* from those sources and the *texture/illustration* from Undivided, rather than double-counting.
6. **Genre/coverage.** This is a *narrative-illustration* source: rich on A2 (belonging), A4 (relationships/bridging), A6 (dynamics/trust), and the spark→vehicle seam; near-silent on A7 decision-making, A8 conflict mechanics, A9 succession, A5 task/goal design. Don't stretch it to fill those — its value is example density on the themes it does carry.
7. **Situation-tag pressure.** Entries cluster naturally on *S:group-feels-transactional*, *S:lost-momentum-after-launch*, *S:no-one-takes-initiative*, and *S:burning-out*. This source is a strong retrieval target for the "we had a great start and it fizzled / it all feels like a transaction / people won't step up" cluster. Note that "The unequal cost" carries **no** situation tag — a first-timer is unlikely to phrase that burden as a felt symptom, which is itself part of the register-gap concern in (2).
