# Difficult Conversations — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Three Conversations ↔ Learning stance** — [A8.1] × [A8.2]: the three-conversations diagnosis is *what* you sort out; the learning stance is the *orientation* you adopt once you have — seeing all three layers is what makes a delivery stance feel pointless. (Entries: "A difficult conversation is really three conversations", "Shift from a message-delivery stance to a learning stance")
- **Different stories ↔ The Third Story** — [A8.1] × [A8.1]: if each person lives in a different story, the Third Story is the neutral frame that names the *difference* without crowning a winner — the diagnosis (two stories) generates the opening move (a third). (Entries: "We each live in a different story", "Begin from the Third Story")
- **Intent vs. impact ↔ Blame vs. contribution** — [A8.1] × [A8.5]: both replace a backward-looking verdict with forward-looking understanding — invented intent fuels blame, so disentangling intent is a precondition for mapping contribution instead. (Entries: "Disentangle impact from intention", "Abandon blame; map the joint contribution system")
- **The And Stance ↔ Ground a complex identity** — [A8.5] × [A1.6]: the And Stance turned *outward* holds two people's stories as both legitimate; turned *inward* it holds "I erred *and* I'm capable" — the same move stabilises a conflict and a self. (Entries: "The And Stance", "Drop all-or-nothing; ground a complex identity")
- **Feelings leak ↔ Find the feeling under the judgment** — [A8.1] × [A8.5]: unexpressed feelings leak *because* they've been converted into judgments; recovering the feeling under the judgment is how you express them cleanly instead of leaking them. (Entries: "Feelings are at the heart — and unexpressed, they leak", "Find the feeling under the judgment")
- **Listening ↔ Expression** — [A4.3] × [A8.2]: the book's two hardest skills are reciprocal — people who can't express themselves can't listen, so authentic listening and clear, view-not-truth expression unlock each other. (Entries: "Listening transforms the conversation", "Express yourself: you're entitled")
- **Acknowledge ≠ agree ↔ Take the lead / problem-solve** — [A8.5] × [A7.2]: acknowledging feelings clears the way to substance, which is the precondition for joint problem-solving — you reframe and invent options *after* the feeling is heard, not instead of it. (Entries: "Inquiry, paraphrase, acknowledgment", "Take the lead: reframe, and problem-solve jointly")
- **The difficult member ↔ Name the dynamic** — [A8.4] × [A8.2]: when listening and problem-solving fail against a bully or derailer, naming the dynamic is the escalation-proof tool that exposes their implied rule and protects the group's ability to decide on merits. (Entries: "When a member is genuinely difficult", "Name the dynamic")
- **Avoid-or-confront ↔ Whether to raise it** — [A8.3] × [A1.5]: the dilemma is the *felt* version (raise it and risk rupture, avoid it and rot); "whether to raise it" is the *deliberate* version that resolves it — sometimes by changing your own contribution rather than confronting. (Entries: "The avoid-or-confront dilemma", "Whether to raise it — or change your own contribution instead")
- **Invite, don't impose ↔ Ownership-and-voice** — [A8.5] × [A3.2]: an invitation that offers an appealing role works *because* people commit to what they help shape — consent-based engagement and ownership are the same mechanism seen from the conflict and motivation sides. (Entries: "Invite, don't impose", and cross-corpus to A3.2 motivation entries)
