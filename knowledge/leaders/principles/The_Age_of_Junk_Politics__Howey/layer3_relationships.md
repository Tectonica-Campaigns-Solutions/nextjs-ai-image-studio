# The Age of Junk Politics — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Transaction/transformation ↔ The ask** — [A2.5] × [A3.3]: the mode of a group shows up most concretely in how its asks are made — extractive asks express the transactional mode, relational asks the transformational. (Entries: "Transactional vs. transformational, in how you treat your people", "The relational ask vs. the extractive ask")
- **Participation ↔ Belief/ownership** — [A3.2] × [A3.4]: people form beliefs and deepen commitment by taking part, so participation is the route to ownership rather than its reward. (Entries: "People form beliefs by taking part", "Transformation takes time, patience, and love")
- **Belonging ↔ Belief** — [A2.4] × [A3.2]: belonging is "primary to belief" — people align with the groups they feel part of, so the felt "we" precedes and drives the shift in conviction. (Entries: "Belonging is primary to belief", "People form beliefs by taking part")
- **Relationships ↔ Resilience** — [A4.1] × [A10.5]: a group bound by relationships absorbs shocks a message-and-money group can't (Bloomberg's collapse), so relationship is what carries a group through adversity. (Entries: "Relationships are the material of the work", "Hope, and the leader as a symbol of collective values")
- **Personal contact ↔ Relationships** — [A4.2] × [A4.1]: the more personal the interaction, the more transformative — so the 1:1 is the highest-yield relational practice, depth beating reach. (Entries: "Get personal: depth over reach", "Relationships are the material of the work")
- **Two engines ↔ Belonging** — [A3.1] × [A2.4]: participation (doing/agency) plus human contact (connection/belonging) are the paired ingredients of transformation; neither alone suffices. (Entries: "The two engines", "Belonging is primary to belief")
- **Public narrative ↔ Shared purpose** — [A2.2] × [A2.1]: speaking from the self (story of self/us/now) is how shared purpose is carried into a room through a personal, relational channel. (Entries: "Be human: speak from the self", "Shared purpose as the underlying driver")
- **Power-with ↔ Developing leaders** — [A7.1] × [A9.1]: power-with grows total power by liberating others' agency, which is the same act as developing leaders — you can't do one without the other. (Entries: "Power-with, not power-over", "Build leadership at every level")
- **Decentralized decisions ↔ Founder bottleneck** — [A7.3] × [A9.3]: ceding real decisions is what lets leadership grow and what breaks the everything-routes-through-me bottleneck. (Entries: "Decentralize decisions so leadership can grow", "Build leadership at every level")
- **Experiential capacity ↔ Learning loop** — [A9.6] × [A6.6]: capacity is built by doing plus reflection, so the debrief is the engine that turns experience into leadership. (Entries: "Capacity is built experientially, not in a training", "Listen up the chain")
- **Members' intelligence ↔ Shared decisions** — [A6.6] × [A7.2]: the frontline experience members gather is intelligence that should inform the group's decisions, linking learning to deciding. (Entries: "Listen up the chain", "Capacity is built experientially")
- **Shared purpose ↔ Stall patterns** — [A2.1] × [A6.4]: "the group lost its why" is the classic stall — purpose going unspoken while tasks take over — so re-naming purpose is the reliable reset. (Entries: "Shared purpose as the underlying driver", "Transactional vs. transformational")
- **Pay ↔ Intrinsic motivation** — [A3.6] × [A3.1]: extrinsic reward can crowd out the shared purpose that makes work transformational, so pay must be paired with purpose-alignment. (Entries: "Pay can crowd out purpose", "The two engines")
- **Mobilizing/organizing ↔ Relationships** — [A2.5] × [A4.1]: a "mobilizing" task becomes transformational only when its human-connection core is honored — the label doesn't decide it, the relationship does. (Entries: "Mobilizing vs. organizing", "Relationships are the material of the work")

## Flags (resisted clean single-domain assignment)

- **The transactional ↔ transformational distinction** is the book's whole spine and a register *lens* (A2.5). The register says tag a lens "only when the chunk is explicitly about it" — here many chunks genuinely are, so A2.5 is used as a primary tag more than for any other source. Watch for over-concentration at A2.5 when pruning; several entries could lean on their secondary code (A3.3, A4.1, A9.1) instead.
- **Dialogical vs. dialectical conversation** (mutual change vs. one-side-wins) is a sharp, useful idea with no dedicated leaf; it's folded into "Be human"/relationships and tagged inline near A4.1/A2.2. Candidate glossary entry, not its own code.
- **The "Organising Equation"** (trust, relationships, belonging, identity, objective alignment, hope, narrative, leadership & symbolism) is a Tectonica list spanning A2, A4, A7, A10 — captured across "Belonging is primary" and "Hope" with Source-reach notes rather than forced into one leaf.
- **Distributed / big organizing** straddles the A7.3/A9.1 leader-principle (ceding decisions to grow leaders) and A7.4/H:org-level (program-level structure design). Split deliberately: principle stays, structure design routes to H:org-level.
- **Author/provenance:** this is Tectonica founder Ned Howey's own book — treat as a flagship statement of house philosophy, but keep attribution honest where it leans on others (Burns, Ganz, Han & McKenna, Gerber & Green, Alinsky, brown, bell hooks), several of whom are their own corpus sources.

## Coverage gaps (leaves this source barely or never feeds)

- **A5 (Group Design & Structure)** — almost nothing: the book is about *moving people*, not structuring a working team (composition, size, task design, norms, boundaries). Use Fisher for A5.
- **A8 (Conflict, Dissent & Accountability)** — thin: "conflict" appears only as a transformational driver (the mother's confrontation), not as task/relationship conflict, dissent, dominating members, or peer accountability. Use Fisher/Alinsky for A8.
- **A6 (Group Dynamics & Lifecycle)** — partial: feeds A6.3 (belonging), A6.6 (learning), A6.2 (slow arc) lightly; nothing on norms-into-culture (A6.1) or psychological safety (A6.5).
- **A10 (Sustaining People)** — light: hope/resilience (A10.5) and symbolism touch A10.3; no real treatment of burnout, self-care, or collective care.
- **A1 (Inner Game)** — indirect: power-with implies A1.1/A1.2 but the book never addresses the leader's identity, imposter feeling, or fear of asking head-on.

## Excluded from this summary

Cut as out of scope for a volunteer group leader (logged for review):

- **Society/industry diagnosis kept only as orienting notes:** the full junk-politics analysis, the "cringe-culture revolt," Citizens United, the political-industry economics, social-media/Big-Tech critique, and the AI-threat argument (ch.1, parts of ch.3.5) — society-level, not leader-facing.
- **Campaign-strategy mechanics:** detailed persuasion/mobilization/resource model graphics and their internal tradeoffs, polling's decline, segmentation/integration structures, "measuring transformational value" at campaign scale (ch.2.2, 3.5, 3.6) — route to **H:org-level**.
- **Tactical how-to:** canvassing logistics, route/script design, deep-canvassing technique, timing of programs, peer-to-peer SMS/phone-banking mechanics (ch.3.7–3.9) — route to **H:events** / **H:comms** / **H:recruitment**; only the why-level was kept.
- **Vendor/tool content:** all Ecanvasser product descriptions and the Georgia (EECMD), Northern-Europe-party, and Ecanvasser case studies — tool-specific, dropped.
- **Campaign illustrations** used only where they carried a kept concept (Bloomberg → fragility without relationships; Corbyn → mobilizing-without-transformation); the rest of the campaign-by-campaign commentary (Brexit, Trump 2am tweets, Bernie/Beto big-organizing, climate/BLM at movement scale) dropped as society/movement altitude.
