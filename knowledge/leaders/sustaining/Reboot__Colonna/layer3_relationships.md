# Reboot — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Complicity ↔ The shadow** — [A1.6] × [A1.4]: complicity is the shadow made actionable — the parts of ourselves we disown create the conditions we then complain about. (Entries: "How am I complicit...", "The shadow and projection")
- **The shadow ↔ The Irrational Other** — [A1.4] × [A8.1]: we meet our disowned selves projected onto the people who most irritate us; the difficult other is a mirror. (Entries: "The shadow and projection", "The Irrational Other and difficult conversations")
- **The leader's inner state ↔ Group culture** — [A1.3] × [A6.1]: the leader's unexamined patterns get replicated and amplified into the group's culture; the mirror runs both ways. (Entries: "The leader's inner state shapes the group's culture", "Buried vs productive conflict")
- **Radical self-inquiry ↔ Standing still** — [A1.4] × [A10.2]: you cannot inquire while spinning, so stillness is the precondition for self-inquiry. (Entries: "Radical self-inquiry", "Standing still")
- **The crucible ↔ Resilience as path** — [A1.6] × [A10.5]: the crucible is where resilience is forged; equanimity is the far side of it. (Entries: "The crucible", "Resilience is the path; equanimity is the goal")
- **The busyness trap ↔ Standing still** — [A10.1] × [A10.2]: doing-as-flight is the disease, deliberate stillness the medicine. (Entries: "The busyness trap", "Standing still")
- **Strong back, open heart ↔ Vulnerability invites care** — [A1.5] × [A6.5]: the open-heart half of the stance is what lets the leader's honesty open the group to caring for one another. (Entries: "Strong back, open heart", "Vulnerability invites collective care")
- **The Crow ↔ The comparison trap** — [A1.4] × [A1.4]: the inner critic feeds on comparison to idealized others; both keep the leader out of their seat. (Entries: "The Crow", "The comparison trap")
- **Ghosts / inherited scripts ↔ The Irrational Other** — [A1.4] × [A8.1]: inherited relational rules ("don't upset the Other") drive how we handle conflict, often unconsciously. (Entries: "Ghosts in the machine", "The Irrational Other and difficult conversations")
- **Fear / the Loyal Soldier ↔ Taking your seat** — [A1.5] × [A1.4]: fear keeps the leader out of the seat; claiming the seat is the move made in fear's presence. (Entries: "Fear as the block to action", "Taking your seat")
- **Purpose through self-inquiry ↔ Radical self-inquiry** — [A2.1] × [A1.4]: the group's truest why often hides in the leader's own history, and inquiry is what surfaces it. (Entries: "Purpose discovered through self-inquiry", "Radical self-inquiry")
- **Enable, don't save ↔ "Enough" / ceding control** — [A1.2] × [A9.4]: not-rescuing and letting-go are the same muscle — both require the leader to stop being defined by doing. (Entries: "Enable and bear witness", "'Enough': equanimity and ceding control")
- **Facing reality ↔ Vulnerability invites care** — [A1.6] × [A6.5]: naming the true, hard thing aloud is what lets the group face it together instead of being blindsided. (Entries: "Facing reality as it is", "Vulnerability invites collective care")

## Excluded from this summary

- **Venture-capital and finance machinery** — capital raising, equity, board governance, term sheets, IPO-vs-acquisition decisions. Off-altitude for a volunteer group leader; dropped (not routed, since it isn't even org-program design the coach would orient to).
- **The executive-firing / CEO-succession mechanics** — kept only as *principle* (the crucible, ceding control); the role-specific process is dropped.
- **The memoir as biography** — Colonna's detailed family history, childhood scenes, and life chronology are used only where a beat illustrates a transferable concept; the rest is left in the book.
- **The clinical / medical detail** — including the author's account of past self-harm and a cardiac scare — referenced only at the why-level for the depletion-to-equanimity thread, non-graphically and with no specifics (see Sensitive-content handling). The detail itself is excluded.
- **Extended poetry, Buddhist, and scriptural passages** — Colonna reproduces poems (Palmer, Whyte, Rich, O'Donohue) and expounds Buddhist and Christian teachings at length. The *transferable teaching* is kept (Milarepa, basic goodness, "place the words on the heart"); the reproductions and the theology beyond the leadership point are excluded.
- **The peer-sharing "boot camp" mechanics** — the third term of the opening formula (sharing the experience with peers) is kept as principle inside the vulnerability and collective-care entries; the design and logistics of running such a gathering are not the leader's concern here and are dropped.
