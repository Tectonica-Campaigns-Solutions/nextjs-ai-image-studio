# The Collective Edge — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Structure ↔ Process ↔ Effectiveness** — [A5.4] × [A6.4] × [A10.6]: structure (tasks/composition/goals/norms) shapes process (effort + coordination), which produces effectiveness (task performance + satisfaction), which loops back — the book's spine. (Entries: "Design motivating work", "Structure before coaching", "Effectiveness has two halves")
- **Structure-before-process ↔ Relaunch** — [A6.4] × [A5.7]: because stalls are usually structural, the reliable fix is to relaunch and reset structure, not coach harder. (Entries: "Structure before coaching", "Relaunch when the group stalls")
- **Group size ↔ Decision quality** — [A5.2] × [A7.2]: past ~7 members a group can't hold a real discussion, so right-sizing is a precondition for good decisions. (Entries: "Group size: the 3-to-7 real team", "Lead the how of decisions")
- **Composition ↔ Productive dissent** — [A5.1] × [A8.2]: deep-level diversity only yields better decisions once dissent is surfaced rather than suppressed. (Entries: "Compose for deep-level diversity", "Productive dissent")
- **Psychological safety ↔ Productive dissent** — [A6.5] × [A8.2]: safety is the precondition that lets unique information and disagreement reach the table. (Entries: "Psychological safety", "Productive dissent")
- **Psychological safety ↔ Learning together** — [A6.5] × [A6.6]: honest reflection and error-reporting only happen where it's safe to admit mistakes. (Entries: "Psychological safety", "Learning together")
- **Conformity ↔ Diversity/boundaries** — [A8.2] × [A5.6]: groups narrow toward a shared reality over time, so semi-porous boundaries and newcomers are the inoculation against conformity. (Entries: "Going along to get along", "Stay open at the edges")
- **Norms ↔ Launch** — [A5.5] × [A5.7]: norms form fastest and stick hardest at the start, so launch is the cheapest moment to set healthy ones. (Entries: "How norms form", "Launch: where structure becomes process")
- **Goals ↔ Autonomy/direction** — [A5.3] × [A7.3]: clear directed goals plus autonomy on method is the motivational sweet spot. (Entries: "Good goals", "Direct the what, leave autonomy on the how")
- **Task vs relationship conflict ↔ Belonging** — [A8.1] × [A6.3]: relationship conflict is uniquely damaging because it attacks belonging, the reason people joined. (Entries: "Task conflict vs relationship conflict", "Effectiveness has two halves")
- **Competition ↔ Relationship conflict** — [A7.1] × [A8.1]: competition motivates the confident but curdles into status games and personal conflict, especially for the still-learning. (Entries: "Competition cuts both ways", "Task conflict vs relationship conflict")
- **Cross-cutting ties ↔ Shared purpose** — [A4.6] × [A2.1]: ties built before friction plus a superordinate goal are what keep sub-groups from turning on each other. (Entries: "Build cross-cutting ties", "Make purpose and goals explicit")
- **Functional shared leadership ↔ Founder bottleneck** — [A9.5] × [A9.3]: spreading leadership acts across members is the direct cure for everything routing through one person. (Entries: "Functional, shared leadership", "Down with the hero")
- **Relationships ↔ Cooperation** — [A4.3] × [A6.3]: trust and cohesion are as much a *result* of cooperating well as a cause — so do the work, don't force the bonding. (Entries: "Relationships follow cooperation", "Launch: where structure becomes process")
- **Hierarchy ↔ Shared leadership** — [A7.6] × [A9.5]: keeping hierarchy "just enough" is what lets leadership flex to whoever has the relevant skill. (Entries: "Just enough hierarchy", "Functional, shared leadership")
- **Idiosyncrasy credits ↔ Peer accountability** — [A8.2] × [A8.3]: informal standing (loyalty + consistency) is the currency a member without authority spends to influence peers. (Entries: "How a minority influences the majority", "Productive dissent")
- **Task design ↔ Satisfaction/retention** — [A5.4] × [A10.6]: whole, meaningful, visible work is what keeps volunteers showing up, linking the structural and relational halves. (Entries: "Design motivating work", "Effectiveness has two halves")

## Flags (resisted clean single-domain assignment)

- **"Collective perspective"** (seeing groups not individuals; fundamental attribution error, egocentric bias) is the book's master frame but has no dedicated leaf or Layer 0 principle of its own; it is distributed across "Structure is king" and "Leadership is functional and shared" and tagged to [A9.5]/[A1.2] where it surfaces. Candidate for a future foundational entry if it recurs across sources.
- **Synergy / process losses (effort + coordination)** is foundational vocabulary, folded into "Structure is king" (cultivate conditions, not causes) and tagged to the structural leaves it governs ([A5.2]/[A5.4]). Worth a glossary entry rather than its own code.
- **Structure-before-process** is the cross-cutting lens already expressed by the A5↔A6 split (the coach's first diagnostic question); it is the "Structure is king" baseline in practice, tagged at [A6.4]/[A5.7], not given its own code (see Q13).

## Coverage gaps (leaves this source barely or never feeds)

The book is rich on A5 (structure), A6 (dynamics), A7–A8 (power/decisions/conflict), and A9.5 (shared leadership), but thin or silent on much of the *relational-organizing* and *sustaining* material this eCoach also needs. Note for RAG balance — feed these leaves mainly from other sources:

- **A1 (inner game):** A1.1 earned authority/consent, A1.4 imposter feeling, A1.5 fear of asking, A1.6 failure-as-learning — little direct treatment.
- **A2:** A2.2 public narrative (Ganz), A2.3 values-as-glue, A2.5 mobilizing-vs-organizing — not covered (Fisher's frame is task performance + satisfaction, not organizing).
- **A3:** A3.4 commitment-over-time and A3.5 the-ask/escalating-commitment (→H:recruitment), A3.6 money-and-motivation — only glancing.
- **A4:** A4.1 relationships-are-strategy, A4.2 the-1:1-relational-meeting, A4.5 recruiting-as-relational (→H:recruitment) — the relational-organizing core is absent; Fisher treats relationships as an outgrowth of cooperation, not as the medium of the work.
- **A7.4** the 5-level autonomy framework (org-check) — not in this source.
- **A9:** A9.1 developing-leaders, A9.4 succession-and-ceding-control, A9.6 coaching-group-members — touched only lightly via "shared leadership."
- **A10 (sustaining):** A10.1 burnout, A10.2 leader-self-care, A10.3 celebration/recognition, A10.4 collective-care, A10.5 resilience-through-setbacks — essentially uncovered (only A10.6 satisfaction-and-retention is well served).

## Excluded from this summary

Cut as irrelevant to leading a *small* group, or as org/society altitude, and logged here so the choice is reviewable:

- **Intergroup-war and large-scale conflict narratives:** Hatfield–McCoy feud, Vietnam escalation, Israel–Hamas, the Pig & Potato War of 1859, the dollar-auction story, Robbers Cave/Middle Grove blow-by-blow (principles kept; the war stories cut). The *mechanisms* (escalation of commitment / sunk cost, revenge, vicarious revenge, dehumanization, two-eyes-for-an-eye) are noted only insofar as they inform task-vs-relationship conflict and cross-cutting ties.
- **Societal/political polarization:** the M&M's "spokescandy" furor, QAnon, the Love Has Won cult, acrophily, echo chambers, partisan-news trust gaps. The within-group seed (extreme voices get airtime; race-to-the-poles) is referenced lightly under conformity/dissent; the political and cult material is cut.
- **Metaphor/illustration scaffolding:** superorganisms and ants, the Borg / *Star Trek*, Harry Potter's Sorting Hat, Milgram obedience and the trolley problem, the QWERTY/Dvorak path-dependence aside, the casino/video-poker metaphor (kept only as the "structure vs coaching" point).
- **Technology-futures chapter specifics:** virtual/augmented reality, AI avatars, brain implants, Polaroid and the innovator's dilemma. (The *learning-to-adopt-new-tools* lesson via the MICS surgery teams is kept under "Learning together"; virtual-team norm-stability is folded into communication norms.)
- **Formalizing a group into an organization** is reduced to a single light orienting entry routed to H:org-level; the deeper org-design discussion is out of scope for the volunteer leader.
