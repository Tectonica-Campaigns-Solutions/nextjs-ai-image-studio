# Thinking in Systems — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

How the entries tie together. One row per relationship.

- **Structure ↔ Behavior** — [A6.4] × [A8.1]: behavior is latent in structure, so a recurring problem is read off the wiring, not the people; this is why blame and personnel swaps fail. (Entries: "Your group is a system", "Bounded rationality")
- **Stocks ↔ Flows/Delays** — [A4.3] × [A6.2]: a stock (trust, momentum) only moves at the speed its flows fill or drain it, and delays make the change lag — so patience and flow-management beat willing the level up. (Entries: "Stocks", "Flows, delays, and why change takes time")
- **Reinforcing loop ↔ Founder bottleneck** — [A9.3] × [A1.2]: the over-helpful leader is a reinforcing loop; naming the loop reveals that the cure is taking *less*, not trying harder. (Entries: "Reinforcing loops", "Trap — Shifting the burden")
- **Balancing loop ↔ Stuck group** — [A6.4] × [A7.5]: a balancing loop defends a set point, so pushing harder meets matching resistance (policy resistance); the move is surfacing competing goals, not force. (Entries: "Balancing loops", "Trap — Seeking the wrong goal")
- **Resilience ↔ Self-organization** — [A10.5] × [A7.3]: redundancy protects bounce-back, and both are destroyed by the same over-control; distributing capacity serves both at once. (Entries: "Resilience", "Self-organization")
- **Over-control ↔ Hierarchy** — [A7.6] × [A7.3]: structure exists to serve the small units, so any layer beyond what coordination needs starves self-organization. (Entries: "Just-enough hierarchy", "Self-organization")
- **Bounded rationality ↔ Information flows** — [A3.2] × [A5.5]: behavior is bounded by what each actor sees, so changing the information (not the person) is the high-leverage, low-coercion fix. (Entries: "Bounded rationality", "Information flows as leverage")
- **Shifting the burden ↔ Friction by Design** — [A9.3] × [A1.2]: the addiction/dependence trap is the systems mechanism Tectonica's designed friction guards against — keep the group's own capacity working. (Entries: "Trap — Shifting the burden", "Dancing with systems")
- **Tragedy of the commons ↔ Success to the successful** — [A10.1] × [A9.2]: two distribution traps — one erodes the reliable few, the other concentrates capability in the star; both starve the wider group and both yield to deliberate load-sharing. (Entries: "Trap — Tragedy of the commons", "Trap — Success to the successful")
- **Drift to low performance ↔ Goal-design** — [A6.4] × [A5.3]: letting standards track recent (badly-perceived) performance creates a downward loop on the goal itself; holding the bar absolute, or anchoring on best performance, reverses it. (Entries: "Trap — Drift to low performance", "Trap — Seeking the wrong goal")
- **Seeking the wrong goal ↔ Goals/paradigm leverage** — [A5.3] × [A2.1]: a system delivers what its goal rewards, so the highest-leverage repair for a hollowed-out group is re-pinning the goal to purpose. (Entries: "Trap — Seeking the wrong goal", "Goals and paradigm")
- **Leverage ladder ↔ Look below the event** — [A5.5] × [A6.4]: events tempt low-leverage pushes; pulling back to structure is what lets a leader find the high rungs. (Entries: "The leverage ladder", "Look below the event")
- **Dancing with systems ↔ Self-organization** — [A6.6] × [A7.3]: watch-before-you-intervene humility is what leaves room for the group to organize itself and for the leader to build judgment rather than substitute it. (Entries: "Dancing with systems", "Self-organization")

## Flags

- **`register-gap` (systems machinery has no Axis A home).** Entries tagged to nearest leaf + flagged: "Your group is a system" (the lens), "Flows, delays…" (delay), "Reinforcing/Balancing loops" (loop), "Look below the event" (levels-of-seeing), "The leverage ladder" (leverage). All tagged to A6.4 or adjacent leaves as nearest fit; **no codes invented.** See the orientation MARK for the recommended fix (add a *systems thinking / structure-produces-behavior* cross-cutting lens; do not add a domain).
- **Cross-cutting lens overlap.** Meadows is effectively the theory under the existing *structure-before-process* lens (the A5 vs A6 split). Several Layer 0 principles read as that lens stated in feedback-loop terms. If you add the systems lens, decide whether it *replaces*, *subsumes*, or *sits beside* structure-before-process.
- **`A2.5` transactional-vs-transactional lens** surfaces inline twice (seeking the wrong goal; goals/paradigm) but is never a header tag, per the lens rule — flagged so retrieval can see the connection.
- **Friction by Design tie.** "Shifting the burden" and "Dancing with systems" are the strongest corpus-internal links to the Tectonica *Friction by Design* document. If the coach is meant to cite that posture, these two entries are the bridge.

## Coverage gaps

- **No small-group evidence.** Meadows offers essentially zero examples at 3–7 person group scale; every group application here is our re-grounding of a scale-free mechanism. Throughout, the coach should attribute the *concept* to Meadows ("in *Thinking in Systems*, Meadows argues…") and own the *group framing* as the coach's, rather than implying Meadows wrote about volunteer groups.
- **Leverage-ladder ordinals are provisional.** Meadows herself hedges the exact rank order. The coach should teach the *direction* (low = numbers/effort; high = information/rules/goals/paradigm), not defend the precise 12-step sequence.
- **Conflict is thin.** Meadows touches conflict only via escalation (excluded) and bounded rationality. For two-member clashes and dominating members (A8.x), other corpus sources carry the weight; Meadows contributes only the "don't blame the person, look at the structure" reframe.

## Excluded from this summary

Cut as out-of-altitude or out-of-scope for leading a small distributed-organizing group (logged so the choice is reviewable):

- **The entire "Systems Zoo" (ch.2)** worked examples: thermostat, coffee cooling, capital-stock economy, oil/non-renewable stock, fishery/fishing-fleet model, population/demographic models. *Concepts kept (loops, delays, oscillation); examples dropped.*
- **Macro/policy/ecosystem cases** throughout: national economies, OPEC/oil pricing, GNP, monetary and market dynamics, climate/ozone, spruce-budworm forest dynamics, Soviet/East-German economic transition.
- **The escalation trap** (arms races / competitive one-upping) — thin fit for a 3–7 person group; conflict spirals route to A8.1 / A8.4.
- **Most of the ch.7 "dancing" guidelines** at analyst/society scale (e.g., "expand the boundary of caring," "expand time horizons," "make feedback policies / meta-feedback," "go for the good of the whole," "celebrate complexity," "don't erode the goal of goodness," language-pollution discussion) — distilled to the four transferable to a small-group leader.
- **The technical/modeling apparatus:** stock-and-flow diagram conventions, the equations appendix, the modeling-and-simulation methodology, the systems-resources bibliography.
- **The sustainability / *Limits to Growth* framing** and the planetary-stewardship motivation that bookends the text — Meadows' purpose, not the coach's.
