# Handbook of Leadership Theory — ## Layer 1 — The Overall Argument (edited-volume map: editors' five dualities)


**1. The Impact of Leadership — performance vs. meaning.** The editors stage the field's central doubt (does leadership even move outcomes?) and answer that its significance lies less in direct performance than in shaping context and meaning. *Kept:* Hackman's reframe — viability and conditions (ch.4). *Dropped:* variance-partitioning of CEO impact (ch.2, org altitude).
concepts: [A5] [A10.6] [A2.1] · lens: structure-before-process

**2. The Theory of Leadership — the person vs. the role/function.** Eight disciplines split between leader-as-special-person and leader-as-function-in-relationships; the editors keep the tension open. *Kept:* group-level psychology — the three team-leader tasks and three capabilities (Chatman & Kennedy, ch.6). *Dropped:* the OB review (ch.5), sociology (ch.8), economics (ch.9–10), history (ch.11). *Deferred:* power (Nye, ch.12 — overlaps Moray).
concepts: [A1.4] [A9.5] [A5.5] [A6.3]

**3. The Variability of Leadership — core vs. contingent.** No one best way; the question is what is universal vs. situational. *Kept:* directive-vs-participative by task certainty and follower expectations (Lorsch, ch.15) — anchors *Diagnose, then flex*. *Dropped:* cross-cultural/GLOBE (ch.13, global-org altitude). *Deferred:* the competence/likability double bind (Ely & Rhode, ch.14).
concepts: [A7.3] [A1.4] [A9.1]

**4. The Practice of Leadership — agency vs. constraint.** Leaders must act despite limits — time, constituencies, difficult followers. *Kept:* the four ironies of leadership *teams* (Wageman & Hackman, ch.17); decisions as a learnable, error-prone function (Useem, ch.18); "a world people want to belong to" and leading *from behind* (Hill et al., ch.21). *Dropped:* the CEO role in complex orgs (ch.16 → `H:org-level`); globalising leadership (ch.20 → org). *Overlap, not re-summarised:* social-movement leadership (Ganz, ch.19 — already in corpus).
concepts: [A5.2] [A7.2] [A2.4] [A9.5] [A8.4]

**5. The Development of Leaders — knowing/doing vs. being.** All authors land on "made, not born," splitting between competencies (knowing/doing) and identity (being). *Kept:* identity-based development (Ibarra, Snook & Guillén Ramo, ch.22) and authentic-leadership development (Avolio, ch.25) — fills the thin A1 domain. *Dropped:* org development-programme design (ch.24 → `H:org-level`). *Deferred:* the experience conundrum (ch.23), adult development (ch.26).
concepts: [A1.4] [A1.6] [A9.1] [A9.6]
