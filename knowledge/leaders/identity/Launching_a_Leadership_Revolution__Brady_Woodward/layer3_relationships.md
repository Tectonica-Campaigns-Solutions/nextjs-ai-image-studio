# Launching a Leadership Revolution — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Influence-is-earned ↔ Leadership-is-the-limitation** — [A1.1] × [A9.3]: because influence is earned and exercised through the leader's own presence and credibility, the leader's reach is the group's ceiling — which is exactly why Level 4 (developing others) is needed to break it. (Entries: "Leadership is influence, earned — not positional", "Developing Leaders (Level 4)")
- **The Three Hs ↔ Talent-scouting** — [A1.4] × [A9.1]: the same Three Hs that gate the leader's own entry become the first screen for whom the leader develops, so the qualities a leader builds in themselves are the qualities they look for in others. (Entries: "Teachability and the roadblocks to learning", "Talent-scouting")
- **Trilateral Ledger ↔ Relationships-are-strategy** — [A4.1] × [A1.6]: because the three dimensions multiply, a relationship "zero" nullifies character and competence — making relationship-building a structural necessity, not a soft skill. (Entries: "Self-mastery first — the Trilateral Ledger", "People don't follow expertise until they feel you care")
- **Three Levels of Motivation ↔ Casting vision** — [A3.1] × [A2.1]: purpose is both the deepest motivator and the content of vision, so vision-casting is how a leader supplies the most durable fuel to a group. (Entries: "The Three Levels of Motivation", "Casting vision")
- **Servant leadership ↔ Leading (Level 3)** — [A1.1] × [A1.2]: serving people's capacity is how a Level-3 leader gets results through a team without simply doing the work themselves. (Entries: "Servant leadership", "Leading (Level 3)")
- **Empowerment ↔ Autonomy-vs-micromanagement** — [A9.2] × [A7.3]: giving people the freedom to fail or fly is the same move as guiding rather than governing — both require the leader to cede control to grow leaders. (Entries: "Empowering leaders", "Autonomy vs micromanagement")
- **Emotional bank account ↔ Course correction** — [A4.3] × [A8.5]: hard feedback is a "withdrawal" that only a relationship with surplus goodwill can absorb, so correction depends on prior deposits. (Entries: "The emotional bank account", "Course correction")
- **The mentoring relationship ↔ Peer accountability without authority** — [A9.6] × [A8.3]: mentoring "beyond friendship, short of boss" is precisely how a leader holds someone accountable when they have no formal power over them. (Entries: "The mentoring relationship", "Get the protégé to take responsibility")
- **Heart change ↔ Norms becoming culture** — [A9.6] × [A6.1]: imparting a way of thinking (not just behavior) is how a leader's approach internalizes in others and, repeated across people, hardens into the group's shared culture. (Entries: "Pursue a heart change", "Casting vision")
- **Developing Developers (Level 5) ↔ A vision big enough for many** — [A9.4] × [A2.1]: a vision must be large and leader-independent for leadership to reproduce across generations, so succession and big-vision are two sides of the same Level-5 condition. (Entries: "Developing Developers of Leaders (Level 5)", "A vision big enough for many leaders")
- **Failure-as-tuition ↔ Attitude/resilience** — [A1.6] × [A10.5]: treating setbacks as data and persisting through them are the cognitive and emotional halves of the same stance toward adversity. (Entries: "Failure as tuition", "Attitude conquers circumstances")
- **Recognition ↔ Developing leaders** — [A10.3] × [A9.1]: bestowing credit on others (rather than collecting it) is both a motivator and a deliberate tool for growing leaders, which is why a Level-4 leader gives recognition away. (Entries: "Recognition and celebration", "Developing Leaders (Level 4)")

## Excluded from this summary

- **Historical hagiography as standalone content.** The chapter-end "Historical Example" profiles (New Orleans/Andrew Jackson, Mel Fisher, Benjamin Franklin, Elizabeth I, Theodore Roosevelt, Tom Brady, the full Churchill/Washington/Nelson/Paul narratives) are used as illustration only; a few are retold compactly inside entries where they anchor a concept (Churchill, Washington, Nelson, Kroc, MLK, Paul), and the rest are dropped as decoration with no bearing on leading a small volunteer group.
- **The providential / Christian-destiny framing.** "Destiny," "obedience to a God-given vision," "God governs in the affairs of men," scripture citations as evidence — translated into secular purpose/calling where the underlying point is register-relevant (motivation, vision); the religious apparatus itself is not carried over.
- **Network-marketing "system" tactics.** Promoting "the training system," edifying the organization's upline leadership, the audio/CD/book/"functions" learning diet, and the three-step "announce/explain/promote" — these are org-program and tooling material (route H:org-level / H:comms), not the volunteer leader's understanding work, and would over-weight tactical content here.
- **The "5 Fs" personal-balance wheel** (finances, faith, family, friends, fitness). Whole-life personal coaching of a protégé's private life — outside the scope of leading a group; finances/faith especially carry the book's MLM-and-religious frame.
- **Execution mechanics from the Cycle of Achievement.** The nine goal-setting criteria, game-planning at macro/mini/micro levels, the "dominoes" and "big rocks" prioritization images, brainstorming, and the 80/20 principle — these are personal-productivity *how-to*; only the why-level of goal-design is folded lightly into the vision/goals material (and deep goal/meeting mechanics route to H:events). 
- **Pure individual-success / wealth self-help.** Material dream-building (the convertible, the custom home), "what would you do if you knew you couldn't fail," and the general self-help register — out of scope for a coach about leading *people*, not personal achievement.
