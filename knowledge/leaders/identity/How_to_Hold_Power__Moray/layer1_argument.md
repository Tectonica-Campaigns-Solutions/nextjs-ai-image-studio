# How to Hold Power — ## Layer 1 — The Overall Argument (book order)


**1. You have a body, and it has been shaped.** Moray opens with somatics: you are shaped like a wind-bent cypress by your environment, and you change by working three layers at once — body, narratives, practices. Sets up that leadership change can't be purely cognitive.
concepts: [A1.4] [A1.6]

**2. Living in your body is a leadership advantage.** Feeling your inner state gives access to intuition and better decisions; capacity to feel is built slowly and has limits (overwhelm). Sets up the body as a decision instrument.
concepts: [A1.4] [A10.2]

**3. Tension is protection; support it, don't fight it.** Chronic muscle contractions are protective survival strategies; "supporting the contraction" (allowing it) is what lets it release. Resistance is a contraction too. Sets up self-compassion as method.
concepts: [A10.2] [A1.6]

**4. Operate your nervous system.** Polyvagal basics, the window of tolerance, and the B.B.O.D.Y. practice give the leader tools to regulate. Sets up that a regulated leader can hold power and conflict.
concepts: [A10.2] [A1.4]

**5. Inhabit your power.** The pivot. Defines power, formal vs informal, the four ways of using it, power leaks, and earned vs unearned power (privilege). The spine of the book and of this coach.
concepts: [A7.1] [A1.1] [A7.3]

**6. Practice presence at work.** Build embodiment rituals where you actually lead; presence is how you honor people and read situations.
concepts: [A1.4] [A6.1]

**7. Work with triggers, yours and others'.** Trauma-informed leadership: recognize triggers, regulate, and respond rather than react — your survival response is amplified by the power differential.
concepts: [A10.2] [A8.1] [A6.5]

**8. Boundaries.** Four boundary problems (can't say/hear no, can't say/hear yes); boundaries prevent burnout and resentment; honoring others' no is a gift.
concepts: [A10.2] [A6.5]

**9. Consent and dissent.** Build a culture where "no" is safe; *draw out dissent* to reach real consent; make requests, not demands.
concepts: [A6.5] [A8.2] [A1.5]

**10. Listening and belonging.** Listening (talk ≤20%), allowability of feeling, and belonging as the driver of people staying; receive feedback by mining the nugget of truth.
concepts: [A9.6] [A6.3] [A10.6]

**11. Conflict and repair.** Be an ethical fighter (rules of engagement, time-outs); don't retaliate; repair via owning contributions, acknowledging impact, do-overs, apology/acknowledgy, reparations, reconciliation.
concepts: [A8.1] [A8.5]

**12. Create the culture.** Integration: the leader is "the bigger nervous system in the room," and the practiced inner work compounds into a culture of embodied humanity.
concepts: [A6.1] [A6.5] [A1.3]
