# How to Hold Power — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Leadership is somatic ↔ What power is** — [A1.4] × [A7.1]: people follow a present, regulated body, so embodiment is the source of the informal/earned power a volunteer leader actually has. (Entries: "Leadership is somatic, not positional", "What power is — formal vs informal")
- **Nervous-system regulation ↔ De-escalation / time-out** — [A10.2] × [A8.1]: the same self-regulation that keeps a leader in their window of tolerance is the move that interrupts a co-trigger spiral in conflict. (Entries: "Nervous-system regulation", "De-escalation and the time-out")
- **The body as information ↔ Boundaries** — [A1.4] × [A10.2]: boundaries are first *felt* (the "uh-oh," anger, tightness) before they're named, so the felt sense is the boundary-detection instrument. (Entries: "The body as information", "Boundaries: saying and hearing no")
- **Four ways of using power ↔ Flattening power** — [A7.1] × [A7.3]: power-with is the goal, and flattening is its well-meant failure mode — abdicating judgment to look equal rather than sharing power while still steering. (Entries: "Four ways of using power", "Flattening power")
- **Four ways of using power ↔ Retaliation** — [A7.1] × [A8.1]: revenge in conflict is simply power-over by another name, costing integrity and the relationship. (Entries: "Four ways of using power", "Retaliation: clean vs dirty pain")
- **Consent culture ↔ Drawing out dissent** — [A6.5] × [A8.2]: real consent is unreachable unless the leader actively surfaces the dissent members suppress under the power differential. (Entries: "A culture of consent", "Drawing out dissent")
- **Boundaries ↔ Consent culture** — [A10.2] × [A6.5]: there is no consent without a real no, so a leader's own boundary capacity is the precondition for a culture where members can refuse safely. (Entries: "Boundaries: saying and hearing no", "A culture of consent")
- **A culture of requests ↔ Flattening / delegation** — [A1.5] × [A9.2]: requests (vs demands) are how a leader hands off real ownership without either dominating (power-over) or abdicating (flattening). (Entries: "A culture of requests", "Flattening power")
- **Listening ↔ Belonging** — [A9.6] × [A6.3]: being listened to (and acted on) is the mechanism by which members come to feel they matter and belong — and therefore stay. (Entries: "Listening as a leadership superpower", "Belonging as the driver of staying")
- **Triggers ↔ Repair** — [A1.6] × [A8.5]: because the leader's triggered reaction is amplified by the power differential, the capacity to regulate is what makes honest repair (owning contributions, acknowledging impact) possible. (Entries: "Triggers and trauma-informed leadership", "Repair: the technologies of coming back")
- **Generative conflict ↔ Repair** — [A8.1] × [A8.5]: conflict only becomes generative *because* of the repair that follows it; the rupture plus skillful repair is what deepens trust. (Entries: "Generative conflict and the ethical fighter", "Repair")
- **Presence ↔ Creating a culture** — [A1.4] × [A6.1]: a leader's daily embodiment, repeated and modeled, is what hardens into the group's culture — culture is presence at scale, not policy. (Entries: "Presence: how a leader is read", "Creating a culture of embodied humanity")
- **Narratives/growth mindset ↔ Internal safety** — [A1.4] × [A1.6]: you can only swap a limiting narrative for a chosen one if the inside of you is safe enough that change isn't self-attack. (Entries: "Narratives, the 'shoulds', and a growth mindset", "Internal safety and self-compassion")

## Excluded from this summary

- **All "Practice Moment" exercises** (the bulk of the book's word count) — somatic technique = execution; captured as *why* only, never reproduced.
- **Body-language / posture how-to** (e.g., uncrossing arms, the "quick power fix" steps) — execution; named as principle only.
- **Positional-HR material** that doesn't transfer to a volunteer leader: contracts as the formal consent vehicle, compensation/benefits, firing, and "Talking about Power at Work" framed around career stakes — reframed or dropped, flagged at the relevant entries.
- **Extended personal/relationship case studies not about leading a group** (e.g., Stan's intimacy story, Mona/Dre's couple dynamics) — used only where they illustrate a leadership concept; the relationship-specific detail is cut.
- **Clinical trauma processing** (PTSD modalities, Somatic Experiencing/EMDR resource lists) — Moray flags these as out of scope and routes to professionals; preserved as a boundary, not summarized as method.
- **Org-wide culture design** — Moray explicitly brackets building an embodied *organization* as beyond the book; routed to **H:org-level**.

## Tagging notes & open questions (for the prune)

1. **Embodiment substrate (FLAG 1).** The felt sense / nervous-system / body-as-information construct underlies a third of the entries with no leaf of its own. Decide post-test whether to (a) keep it distributed across A1.4/A7.1/A10.2 with a third cross-cutting lens ("embodiment / bottom-up change"), or (b) mint a leaf (e.g. A1.7 *embodiment-as-information* or under A10). Recommend deciding after seeing how often retrieval wants it as a primary tag.
2. **Personal boundaries (FLAG 2).** No leaf cleanly fits Moray's "boundaries" (personal limits, not group membership/A5.6). Tagged A10.2 + A6.5. Candidate leaf if boundaries recur as a primary across sources.
3. **A7.1 is overloaded here.** Five power entries land on A7.1 (what-power-is, four-ways, flattening, leaks, retaliation). If A7 splits in the prune, a likely seam is *types/sources of power* vs *using power responsibly (over/under/with/within + leaks)*.
4. **Source genre.** This is the suite's clearest *inner-game/somatic* source; it under-covers register domains A2 (purpose/story), A5 (structure), A6.2 (stages), A7.2/A7.4 (decision mechanics), and A9.1–9.5 (developing/distributing leadership). Expect other sources (e.g. Fisher) to carry those; don't stretch Moray to fill them.
