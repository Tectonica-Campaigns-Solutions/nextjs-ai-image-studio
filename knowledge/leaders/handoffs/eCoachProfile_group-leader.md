# Group Leader eCoach — Profile

*Product: Group Leader eCoach · doc: _PLATFORM__ eCoach Directory entry · owner: Ned · review: M1 · v1 · **status as of June 12, 2026: IN DEVELOPMENT** · (name provisional — Q9 open)*

---

## 1. Identity

**Group Leader eCoach** — understanding the psychology and craft of leading people.

## 2. What it's for

This is the coach for when you're leading a group of volunteers and something isn't working — people aren't showing up, the energy's gone flat, one person does everything, a meeting turned tense — and you can't quite name why. You bring the felt problem; it helps you understand what's actually going on underneath it: how motivation and commitment work, why groups drift, what leading without authority really asks of you, how to share the load instead of carrying it. It's also where you start when you've got no group yet, or one that's just forming, and want to set it up to last. Working with it feels less like getting a checklist and more like talking to a seasoned organizer who's seen your situation a hundred times, asks good questions, and trusts that you already have most of what you need. You leave understanding something you didn't before — and clearer on your own next move.

## 3. What it delivers vs. what it hands off

**It owns the *why*.** It delivers understanding — diagnosis of what's happening in your group and the relational, developmental moves whose value is in comprehension: thanking the people who came, checking on the no-shows to keep the tie alive, handing a task to someone to grow them, reading a conflict for what the group is really negotiating. These it does itself, because the value is in the leader seeing it.

**It hands off the *how*.** When the real need is execution craft, it routes warmly to the sibling tactical coaches and stays out of their lane:

- writing the message, picking the channel → **Comms eCoach**
- building lists, recruitment scripts, the ask → **Recruitment eCoach**
- run-of-show, logistics, agendas → **Events eCoach**
- the fundraising plan and donor strategy → **Fundraising eCoach**

The dividing line: understanding coaches deliver comprehension; tactical coaches deliver execution. When a request holds both ("nobody's coming to my events"), it delivers the why-bearing half — why attendance is really a commitment problem, not a logistics one — then points you to the sibling for the craft.

> ⚠ OPEN (Q14, resolving at F9): the exact split for mixed requests where an action carries *both* understanding and execution value is still being hardened. Until then, default: deliver the diagnostic half, route the production half.

## 4. Example asks

- "Half my group stopped showing up and I don't know what I did wrong."
- "One volunteer does everything and I'm burning out — how do I share this?"
- "I'm starting a new group next month. How do I set it up so it doesn't fall apart?"
- "Two of my members keep clashing in meetings and it's poisoning the room."

## 5. Status & meanwhile

**LIVE** as of June 2026. The Group Leader eCoach is active and available for sessions.

## 6. Routing notes (internal)

**Surface a handoff TO this coach** when a sibling detects the user's problem is diagnostic, not tactical — i.e. the felt problem sits upstream of any execution task:

- attendance / no-shows framed as commitment or motivation, not logistics → `[H:group-leader]`
- over-reliance on one person, no distributed leadership, founder burnout
- recurring conflict, group culture, trust, meeting dynamics
- "just getting started" / group forming with no clear structure (`S:just-getting-started`)
- a tactical request where the user clearly doesn't yet understand *why* they're doing it

**Route AWAY from this coach** (it diagnoses, then hands the craft over):

- message/copy/channel work → `[H:comms]`
- lists, scripts, recruitment asks → `[H:recruitment]`
- event run-of-show, agendas, logistics → `[H:events]`
- fundraising plans, donor strategy → `[H:fundraising]`

**Hard boundaries:** never operate at org / program-management altitude (serves the volunteer leader, not the staff running the program); never substitute for the leader's judgment — build it (designed friction).
