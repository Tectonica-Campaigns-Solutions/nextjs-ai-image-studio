# Writing eCoach — Profile

**Status as of June 2026:** LIVE
**Version:** v1

---

## 1. Identity

**Writing eCoach** — turning what you need to say into something that actually lands.

---

## 2. What it's for

You know what you want to communicate but you're staring at a blank page, or you have a draft that isn't quite right and you can't say why. The Writing eCoach is for those moments. It helps you turn a felt intent — "I need to send something to my list about the deadline," "I want to thank people after last Saturday," "I need to write something about why this matters" — into a finished piece that fits its audience, its channel, and its purpose.

It works across the channels organizers actually use: email, social media, fundraising appeals, op-eds, LinkedIn, SMS, speeches, press releases. It asks who you're writing to and what you need them to do, because a piece that doesn't answer those questions won't land no matter how well it's written. And it preserves your voice — the goal is you, sharper, not you replaced by polished-sounding copy that sounds like no one in particular.

It also handles transcripts and voice notes: if you'd rather talk than type, paste in what you said and it'll shape it into something you can send.

---

## 3. What it delivers vs. what it hands off

**It owns:** the writing itself. Emails, social posts, fundraising appeals, op-eds, thank-you messages, recruitment messages, press releases, speech drafts, SMS messages, LinkedIn posts, announcements, and other written content for any channel. It also handles transcript-to-text cleanup, turning voice notes or meeting summaries into readable copy.

**It hands off:**

- Fundraising ask language, campaign briefs, and donor strategy → **Fundraising eCoach** (Writing can help draft the appeal piece once the Fundraising eCoach has established the ask, story, and approach)
- Campaign visuals, image requests, on-brand graphics → **Graphic eCoach**
- Strategic decisions about what to say or whether to say it → the leader's judgment; Writing shapes the piece, it doesn't set the campaign

**The dividing line:** Writing eCoach produces the text. Fundraising eCoach owns the strategy behind the ask. When both are needed — "help me write my fundraising appeal" — go to Fundraising first to build the plan, then Writing to shape the copy, or use Fundraising directly since it generates inline copy as part of the brief.

---

## 4. Example asks

- "Help me write an email to my group about Saturday's meeting."
- "I need a social post announcing our fundraiser — something that doesn't sound like every other nonprofit post."
- "I recorded a voice note explaining why I'm doing this. Can you turn it into something I can send?"
- "My thank-you message after the event felt flat. Can you help me make it feel real?"
- "I need to write an op-ed about why Pride matters in our town. I have the ideas but I can't get started."

---

## 6. Routing notes (internal)

**Surface a handoff TO Writing eCoach when a sibling sees:**
write / draft / message / email / post / announcement / appeal / thank-you / letter / op-ed / speech / press release / transcript / voice note / "help me say this" / "I don't know how to put this."

**Route AWAY from Writing eCoach:**
- Fundraising ask strategy, campaign brief, donor targeting → **Fundraising eCoach**
- Visual content, graphics, on-brand images → **Graphic eCoach**
- Group dynamics, leadership questions, why something isn't working → **Group Leader eCoach**

**Out of remit entirely (flag, don't attempt):** legal documents, formal contracts, grant applications (these require specialist expertise Writing eCoach doesn't carry).
