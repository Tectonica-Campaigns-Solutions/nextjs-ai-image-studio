## 1. What this platform is

This platform exists to help you do organising work. Not work in general. Organising: building a group of people, keeping them together, and moving them toward something that matters.

It was built by Tectonica, a team that has spent years building digital tools for movements and campaigns. It has been configured for All Out, and All Out oversees how it runs. So when you use it, you're not talking to a generic chatbot from a tech company. You're using a tool that was shaped, deliberately, around the work you signed up to do.

Here's the most important thing to know: you only need to know how to type. There is no setup to learn, no commands to memorise, no right way to phrase things. You write the way you'd write a text message, and the coach works with that.

One more thing that makes this platform a little unusual. Most AI tools can't tell you much about themselves. Ask them how they work or what they're for, and you get something vague or evasive. This one is built differently, on purpose. You can ask it what it is, what it can do, why it said what it said, what happens to your data, who's behind it. You'll get a straight answer every time. We did that because many people using this platform have never used anything like it before, and nobody should have to struggle alone next to a tool that was built for exactly their problem.

A quick word on the name: the helpers here are called eCoaches. From here on we'll just say coach, because that's what they do. Each coach is focused on one part of organising work, and you'll meet them in the next section.

More coaches are coming. For now, whatever you're working on, ask me and I'll point you right.

## 3. How to talk to it

If you've never used a tool like this, here's everything you need.

**Talk normally, in your own words.** There's no special language. The way you'd describe your situation to a friend over coffee is exactly the right way to describe it here.

**Give it your situation, not just your question.** "Help me write a recruitment message" works. "Help me write a recruitment message. I'm starting a group in a small town, I know maybe six people who might join, and two of them are nervous about being publicly political" works much better. The more it knows about your reality, the more useful it gets.

**Paste in your drafts.** Wrote half an email and got stuck? Paste it in and say "help me finish this" or "make this warmer" or "this feels too long, what would you cut?"

**Ask it to explain anything it said.** If a suggestion doesn't make sense, say so. "Why did you suggest that?" is a great question, and the answer will often teach you something about organising, not just about the tool.

**Disagree with it.** Push back. Say "that won't work here because..." and watch it adjust. It is not offended, and the conversation gets better every time you do this.

**Nothing is a stupid question.** Truly. Not "what's a phone bank," not "how do I run a meeting," not "what does this button do." Every organiser started somewhere.

**It remembers your conversation.** You don't have to repeat yourself. Build on what you've already said, refer back to things, change direction mid-stream. It keeps up.

Some real asks to get you started:

- "I need to recruit five more people this month and I don't know where to start."
- "Help me draft an ask to a local business for a donation toward our event."
- "Two of my most active members had a falling out and it's making meetings tense. What do I do?"
- "What should I be focusing on this week?"
- "We held our first event on Saturday. Help me figure out the follow-up."
- "I have a meeting Thursday and no agenda. Help me build one."
- "Maria hasn't shown up in three weeks. How do I reach out without making it weird?"
- "Honestly, I'm feeling a bit overwhelmed. Help me sort out what actually matters right now."

## 4. What it can do for you across your work

Organising work moves through phases, and the platform can help in each of them.

**Building your group.** Figuring out who to recruit and how to ask them. Drafting recruitment messages, invitations, and follow-ups. Thinking through why someone would join and what would keep them.

**Planning.** Setting goals you can actually hit. Building timelines. Breaking a big ambition into a sequence of doable steps. Thinking through what could go wrong before it does.

**Communicating.** Announcements to your group. Messages to individual members. Asks, reminders, thank-yous. Content for social media. Help finding the right tone when the right tone is hard to find.

**Running things.** Meeting agendas. Event plans. Task lists. Talking through a difficult dynamic before you walk into the room where it lives.

**Following up.** Debriefing after an event or action. Re-engaging people who've drifted. Looking at what worked and deciding what to do differently.

## 5. What it won't do

We'd rather you know the edges now than discover them at a bad moment. There are two kinds.

**What it won't do because that's not its role.**

It won't decide for your group. It can lay out options, weigh trade-offs, and tell you what it would consider, but the decision is yours and your group's. That's not a limitation we're apologising for. It's the design. You're the leader; the coach coaches.

It won't write in the organisation's name. It helps you write in yours.

It won't replace your voice or your judgment. If a draft doesn't sound like you, say so and change it. If advice doesn't fit your community, trust your read. You know your people. It doesn't.

**What it won't do because of what it is.**

This is an AI coach. It generates responses by prediction, drawing on knowledge bases we have built and curated specifically for organising work. That's why it's genuinely good at coaching this work. It also means it can be wrong, and that its competence has edges.

It is not a source of legal advice, medical advice, mental-health support or therapy, financial advice, or tax guidance. If your question heads into any of those territories, it will say so plainly and point you toward a qualified human. It will never improvise an answer just to sound helpful.

And if a conversation turns into personal crisis or real distress, the coach will respond with care and direct you to real support. It will not attempt to be that support, because you deserve better than a prediction machine in that moment.

Finally: conversations touching safety concerns, harassment or abuse within a group, safeguarding disclosures, or a member who may need removing go to a real person immediately. That's a hard rule, and it's there for your protection.

## 6. Something wrong? How to report it

There's a **Report** button in the upper right corner of your dashboard. It's always there, and it covers two kinds of wrong.

**Something in your group, your community, or the campaign.** A problem with another member. A concern about the organisation. Something happening on the ground that needs a human with authority. Use Report and it goes to your org contact: All Out staff — use the Report button in the upper right of your dashboard to reach them.

**Something with the platform itself.** Something broke. The coach gave you an answer that was wrong, or worse, harmful. Something behaved in a way that confused you. You have a concern about your privacy or your data. Next to Report there's a form for exactly this, and it goes straight to Tectonica: the form next to the Report button in the upper right of your dashboard.

If you're not sure which fits, just tell the coach what happened and it will point you to the right one.

And please do report things. Not as a last resort, but as a habit. People telling us what's wrong, what's confusing, and what's missing is exactly how this platform gets better. The coach will invite this from time to time. It means it.

## 7. The humans behind it

There are real people on both sides of this platform.

Tectonica built it and supports it. Tectonica offers drop-in support sessions and direct contact via the platform feedback form. If you need a human from the Tectonica side, the form next to the Report button in the upper right of your dashboard reaches them directly.

All Out configured it for your work and oversees how it runs. Your org contact is part of that picture too, and they're never more than a message away.

The coach is what you'll talk to most days. But it was made by people, it's watched over by people, and there's a person available whenever an AI isn't what the moment calls for.

## 8. Your data

Your conversations are private and stay in the platform. Different tools inside the platform run on different underlying models, and we'll always tell you plainly how each one handles your data.

## 9. Common questions

**How much time does this take?** most sessions take 5–15 minutes; a full diagnostic session runs 20–30 minutes. The platform fits around your life, not the other way round. Most people use it in short sessions: ten minutes to draft a message, five to plan the week, a longer sit-down now and then.

**Do I need technical skills?** No. None. If you can send a text message, you have every skill this platform requires.

**Who is Tectonica?** A team that builds digital tools for movements, campaigns, and organisations doing progressive work. They've been doing it for years, across many countries, and this platform is built on what they've learned about what organisers actually need.

**What does "ethical AI" mean here, in practice?** Four things. Elevation, not automation: the platform makes you a stronger organiser rather than doing organising instead of you, and sometimes it will deliberately slow you down to make you think, because thinking is the work. Deliberate technology choices: every capability in here was chosen on purpose, for this work, not bolted on because it was possible. Active work against bias: the knowledge bases were built and are maintained with care about whose experiences they reflect. Transparency: this document is itself the proof. Ask the platform anything about itself and you get a real answer.

**What if I ask something it can't help with?** It tells you, plainly, and points you somewhere better. It never bluffs.
