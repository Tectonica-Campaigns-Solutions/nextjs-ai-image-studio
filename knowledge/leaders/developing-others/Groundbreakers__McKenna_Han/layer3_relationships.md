# Groundbreakers — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Organize-don't-mobilize ↔ Develop-leaders** — [A2.5] × [A9.1]: organizing *is* the work of developing leaders rather than extracting action; the lens and the practice are the same claim at two altitudes. (Entries: "Develop leaders, don't just recruit helpers", "Relationships are the strategy")
- **Relationships ↔ Commitment-over-time** — [A4.1] × [A3.4]: relationships are the medium; commitment is what flows through them and what sustains people when enthusiasm fades. (Entries: "Relationships are the strategy", "Commitment to each other outlasts commitment to the cause")
- **The one-on-one ↔ Public narrative** — [A4.2] × [A2.2]: the story exchange is what makes a one-on-one a relationship-builder rather than small talk. (Entries: "The one-on-one", "Public narrative: story of self, us, and now")
- **Enabling-not-doing ↔ Leader-in-the-middle trap** — [A1.2] × [A9.3]: the principle and its failure mode; doing the work yourself is the bottleneck enabling is meant to prevent. (Entries: "Enabling, not doing", "The 'leader in the middle' trap")
- **Delegate-outcomes ↔ Ownership-and-voice** — [A9.2] × [A3.2]: delegating an *outcome* (not a task) is the mechanism that produces ownership, which is the motivation. (Entries: "Delegate outcomes, not tasks", "Ownership and voice")
- **The snowflake ↔ Interdependent roles** — [A5.2] × [A5.4]: the nested small-team shape and the interdependent roles inside each team are two halves of one structural design. (Entries: "The snowflake: nested small teams", "Interdependent roles")
- **Goal-design ↔ Peer accountability** — [A5.3] × [A8.3]: a clear, shared, visible goal is the precondition for peers holding each other accountable without a boss. (Entries: "Goals that are clear, vivid, and grounded", "Peer accountability through shared, visible goals")
- **Autonomy-vs-direction ↔ Goals-are-collective** — [A7.3] × [A7.5]: "direction on the what" is the collective goal everyone agrees to; "autonomy on the how" is the local freedom under it. (Entries: "Direction on the what, autonomy on the how", "Goals are collective, not individual")
- **Direction-autonomy ↔ Ownership** — [A7.3] × [A3.2]: removing local autonomy (e.g., over-staffing) destroys ownership — the "more staff, less ownership" lesson. (Entries: "Direction on the what, autonomy on the how", "Ownership and voice")
- **Stages-and-development ↔ Common-stall-patterns** — [A6.2] × [A6.4]: most stalls are a phase being misread or a developed team failing to seed new ones. (Entries: "The five phases of a team's development", "Learn by experimenting; failure is information")
- **People-focused/metrics ↔ Peer accountability** — [A10.6] × [A8.3]: metrics drive accountability but, untempered by support, corrode the satisfaction that retains people. (Entries: "People-focused *and* metrics-driven", "Peer accountability through shared, visible goals")
- **Values-as-glue ↔ Norms-become-culture** — [A2.3] × [A6.1]: a named value ("respect, empower, include") becomes culture only through repetition and modeling. (Entries: "'Respect, empower, include'", "How 'respect, empower, include' becomes culture")
- **Build-power-from-people ↔ Relationships** — [A7.1] × [A4.1]: power is organized relationships, which is why the under-resourced can build it. (Entries: "Power comes from people, not from money or message", "Relationships are the strategy")
- **Learn-by-experimenting ↔ Recommitment conversation** — [A6.6] × [A8.5]: experimentation guarantees low points; the recommitment one-on-one is how a leader and team recover from them. (Entries: "Learn by experimenting; failure is information", "The recommitment conversation")

## Coverage gaps (doc-level)
This source is strong on relational organizing, distributed leadership, team structure, motivation-through-ownership, and the autonomy/direction balance. It is **near-silent**, and should not be stretched, on: task-vs-relationship conflict [A8.1], productive dissent / groupthink [A8.2], the dominating or toxic member [A8.4], psychological safety [A6.5], onboarding mechanics [A9.7], and interpersonal rupture-repair between members [A8.5 beyond the single "recommitment" instance]. For these, the coach should lean on other corpus sources (e.g., Fisher, Burns, Han) and attribute Groundbreakers only where it genuinely speaks.

## Excluded from this summary
- **Ch.2 in full** — the history of American field campaigns, party decline, the Dean "Perfect Storm," STOMP, the GOP ground game (kept only as the "parachute / command-and-control" foil).
- **The staff hierarchy** — SD → GED → FD → DFD → RFD → FO org chart and the management chain (org-level program design → H:org-level).
- **Turf-cutting, staffing surges, payroll, and fundraising** — e.g., the "tribulations of turf splitting," the $260M late surge, *Citizens United* anxiety (org/program decisions, not the leader's).
- **Metrics machinery and tooling** — VAN/VoteBuilder, NationalField, the daily/hourly reporting pipeline, the four temporal campaign phases as a *management* system, the persuadability index and microtargeting (the *why* of goals/ownership is kept; the tooling routes to H:org-level / H:comms).
- **Scholarship and democracy framing** — lessons-for-scholarship, study limitations/method, "integrated voter engagement," and the post-campaign OFA 2.0/4.0 history (out of altitude for a volunteer group leader).
- **Data-and-field symbiosis** as a campaign-strategy argument (kept only insofar as it shows organized people, not money or data, are the real lever — see A7.1).
