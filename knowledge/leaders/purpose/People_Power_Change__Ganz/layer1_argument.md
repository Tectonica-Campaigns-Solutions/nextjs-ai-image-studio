# People Power Change — ## Layer 1 — The Overall Argument


Ganz's spine, in his order, with the org-altitude and campaign-operations material compressed and logged in Excluded. Paraphrased throughout.

**1. Practicing democracy is a craft you learn by doing.** Ganz opens by framing the whole book as a *pedagogy of practice*: the way you learn (explain, model, practice, debrief, in a brave space) is what you learn. This installs learning-by-doing and the culture of candid feedback as the ground everything else stands on.
concepts: [A6.6 learning-together] [A1.6 failure-as-learning]

**2. Leadership, organizing, action — and power.** He defines leadership as enabling others to achieve shared purpose under uncertainty (a practice, not a position), defines organizing as a form of leadership asking *who are my people / what change / how do we turn resources into power*, and defines power as relational — an interdependent balance of needs and resources, "with" or "over." This reframes the work around people and around earned, enabling authority.
concepts: [A1.1 leading-by-earned-authority-and-consent] [A1.2 enabling-vs-doing] [A7.1 building-and-using-power] [A2.1 shared-purpose]

**3. The five practices.** Relationship building, storytelling, strategizing, acting, and structuring — each a heart/head/hands practice — are introduced as the components a leader integrates. The book then takes one per chapter.
concepts: [A4.1 relationships-are-strategy] [A2.2 public-narrative]

**4. Relationships.** Ganz argues relationships are the medium of the work, motivated by exchangeable difference and made durable by commitment, and that *civic* relationships (built on shared purpose, not kinship) are democracy's fabric. This sets up commitment and the relational ask as the engine of everything later.
concepts: [A4.1 relationships-are-strategy] [A4.3 trust-building] [A2.5 transformational-vs-transactional]

**5. Storytelling.** Public narrative — self, us, now — is how a leader accesses and shares the values that motivate action, turning a fearful reaction to disruption into an agentic, hopeful response. Story (why) is positioned as the partner to strategy (how).
concepts: [A2.2 public-narrative] [A2.3 values-as-glue] [A2.4 group-identity-and-belonging]

**6. Strategizing.** Strategy is *resourcefulness*, not resources — a verb, a theory of change about how to turn what you have into the power you need, generated by particular (ideally diverse, embedded) people. He gives the six strategic questions (who / what change / theory of change / strategic goal / tactics / timing). *Kept at the leader's altitude; the long UFW worked example is compressed.*
concepts: [A7.1 building-and-using-power] [A2.1 shared-purpose] [A5.1 composition-and-skills]

**7. Action.** Action produces observable change by mobilizing and deploying resources, which depends on motivation (designed into the work) and commitment (asked for, protected, kept). He adds measurement-as-learning — evidence-based organizing, the after-action report — as the engine of improvement.
concepts: [A3.1 intrinsic-motivation] [A3.4 commitment-over-time] [A6.6 learning-together]

**8. Structure.** Structuring is committing to how we work, decide, and hold each other accountable — "the opposite of structure is not space but chaos." Ganz names four tensions (change/continuity, inclusion/exclusion, unity/diversity, parts/wholes) and three ways to manage them (distribute responsibility, facilitate collaboration, dissent-is-not-disloyalty). *The leader-level half is kept; the firms-vs-associations and parts-and-wholes org architecture routes to `H:org-level`.*
concepts: [A5.6 boundaries-and-membership] [A8.2 productive-dissent] [A5.5 norms-and-operating-agreements]

**9. Developmental leadership.** The integrating practice: build a real (bounded, stable, diverse) leadership team, snowflake it outward by developing others, structure development into the work (opportunities, responsibilities, accountability), and sustain it all through coaching. This is presented last because it sits at the center of every other practice.
concepts: [A9.1 developing-leaders] [A5.1 composition-and-skills] [A9.6 coaching-group-members] [A9.5 functional-shared-leadership]
