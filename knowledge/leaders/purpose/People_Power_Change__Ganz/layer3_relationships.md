# People Power Change — Relationships, Flags & Coverage

## Layer 3 — Relationship Map

- **Enabling vs. doing ↔ Distributing leadership** — [A1.2] × [A9.2]: enabling others *is* delegating outcomes; the founder who can't delegate is the leader who never stopped doing. (Entries: "The catalyst who enables rather than does", "Delegate outcomes, not tasks")
- **Public narrative ↔ The strategic goal** — [A2.2] × [A7.1]: a story of *now* is where story (why) and strategy (how) meet, so the committed strategic goal is the content of the narrative's call to act. (Entries: "Public narrative: a story of self, us, and now", "The power theory of change, the power map, and the strategic goal")
- **Relationships are strategy ↔ Mobilizing vs. organizing** — [A4.1] × [A2.5]: organizing's medium is the committed relationship; mobilizing aggregates resources without it, which is why mobilized ties dissolve when the reward stops. (Entries: "Relationships are strategy", "Mobilizing vs. organizing; constituency, not clients")
- **The valuable ask ↔ Commitment compounds** — [A3.3] × [A3.4]: a valuable (not easy) ask creates a real commitment, and real commitments generate larger ones — so the relational ask is the first rung of the ladder. (Entries: "The valuable ask, not the easy ask", "Commitment compounds — and must be kept")
- **Task design ↔ Developing leaders** — [A3.1] × [A9.1]: a well-designed task (significance, identity, variety, autonomy, feedback) is "an entry point to leadership," so motivation design and leadership development are one move. (Entries: "Motivation comes from how the work is designed", "Identify, recruit, and develop leadership")
- **Bounded-stable-diverse team ↔ Who strategizes** — [A5.1] × [A7.2]: the same diverse, embedded composition that makes a real team is what gives it strategic capacity to decide well. (Entries: "The bounded, stable, diverse leadership team", "Who strategizes: strategic capacity from a diverse, embedded team")
- **Power is relational ↔ Strategy is resourcefulness** — [A7.1] × [A7.1]: power is a needs/resources balance, and resourcefulness is how you shift that balance with the resources you have — David's stones over Goliath's sword. (Entries: "Power is relational", "Strategy is resourcefulness, not resources")
- **Decisions as shared process ↔ Dissent is not disloyalty** — [A7.2] × [A8.2]: decision *rules* are what let a group keep dissent alive and still move — diversity improves the decision, the rule lets you act on it. (Entries: "Decisions as a shared process", "Dissent is not disloyalty")
- **Norms only exist in practice ↔ Peer accountability** — [A5.5] × [A8.3]: a norm with no correction redefines itself, so shared, light norm corrections are how peers hold each other accountable without a boss. (Entries: "Norms only exist in practice", "Peer accountability as learning, not policing")
- **Autonomy vs. direction ↔ Delegating outcomes** — [A7.3] × [A9.2]: responsibility delegated without the *authority* to act is just a task; local strategic leadership means direction on the what, autonomy (and authority) on the how. (Entries: "Forest and trees: distributing strategic authority", "Delegate outcomes, not tasks")
- **Coaching ↔ Failure as learning** — [A9.6] × [A1.6]: developmental coaching diagnoses the gap (heart/hands/head) and turns an early, low-stakes failure into a debriefed lesson rather than a verdict. (Entries: "Coaching: the five-step developmental process", "Fall off the bike: failure as learning")
- **Founder bottleneck ↔ Burnout** — [A9.3] × [A10.1]: the hub that everything routes through is the same structure that makes one or two people do all the sacrificing and burn out. (Entries: "Letting go: the founder bottleneck and ceding control", "Burnout: spread the sacrifice")
- **Measurement as learning ↔ Resilience through setbacks** — [A6.6] × [A10.5]: measuring capacity and leadership built (not only the goal) is what lets a group read a loss redemptively and try again stronger. (Entries: "Measurement as learning: evidence-based organizing", "Handling losing: narrating a setback")

## Excluded from this summary

Cut as out of scope for a first-time leader of a small distributed-organizing group, or compressed to the transferable principle; logged so the choice is reviewable.

- **Most of the book's campaign-operations detail.** The long worked example of the UFW's 1965–66 campaign to Sacramento (the Schenley boycott, the pilgrimage, the back-channel negotiations, the grower/Brown maneuvering) and the large field-campaign machinery (1988 California GOTV's three-hundred-organizer multitier structure; Obama's state→region→precinct field architecture) are retold by Ganz at length; kept here only as compressed flagship illustrations, with the *principle* extracted and the operations dropped. **Reason: campaign altitude / execution.**
- **Chapter 6's org-design half.** Firms-vs-associations, the upward/downward flow of authority and resources, three-tiered/federated structures, the parts-and-wholes problem, the iron law of oligarchy (Michels/Lipset/Levy), and the detailed union cases (UAW/UNITE HERE/Teamsters; centralized vs. decentralized union forms) sit above a volunteer group leader's altitude. Routed to `H:org-level` with a light orienting note. **Reason: organization, not the leader's group (Boundary 1).**
- **The mobilizing-vs-organizing *campaign architecture*.** Kept the leader-level *stance* (organize, don't merely mobilize; build constituency, not clients) as the A2.5 lens; dropped the structural treatment of how mobilizing vs. organizing campaigns concentrate or distribute authority across an organization. **Reason: org architecture (Boundary 1).**
- **Deep how-to for tactical practices.** The relational-meeting and house-meeting *scripts and mechanics* (ch.2), the storytelling *craft drills* (story-moment construction exercises, ch.3), the four-Cs *ask scripts* and phone-bank operations (ch.5), and meeting/event *logistics* (ch.6) are kept at why-level only; the step-by-step routes to `H:events`, `H:recruitment`, and `H:comms`. **Reason: execution, not understanding (Boundary 2).**
- **The strategy chapter's tactics and timing apparatus (partial).** The tactic-selection criteria and the temporal-strategy / "entrainment" material are summarized at principle level; the detailed campaign-timing mechanics (kickoff sequencing, peak-by-peak deployment) route toward `H:events`. The *theory of change, power map, and strategic goal* are kept in full as leader-level work. **Reason: tactic/timing execution (Boundary 2); strategy concept retained.**
- **Ganz's autobiography and intellectual lineage as such.** The "Why Me / Why Us / Why Now" memoir framing, the SNCC/UFW/Harvard arc, and the academic-debate material (Jenkins & Perrow on whether the UFW's wins were structural; the social-movement literature) are used only where they supply a flagship example or a named mechanism; the biography itself is not summarized. **Reason: not a small-group mechanism.**
- **No entry for A7.4 (the 5-level autonomy framework).** Ganz offers no five-level scheme; that belongs to the organization's autonomy model, not this source. Left to the org-design source. **Reason: register leaf out of source scope.**
- **Thin leaves folded inline rather than given their own entry:** A1.3 lead-by-example (folded into coaching and the empathetic bridge), A4.2 the 1:1 (why-level inside "Relationships are strategy" and "trust"; how-to routed), A4.4 the relationship-web and A4.5 recruiting-as-relational (inside "strong/weak ties" and "the ladder", routed), A6.2 stages-and-development (inside the campaign-arc note and "launch-and-relaunch"), A6.3 cohesion and A6.5 psychological-safety (inside "launch with heart", "celebrate", "coaching", "dissent"), A7.5 collective-agreement-tension (inside "boundaries" and "decisions"), A8.4 the-dominating-or-toxic-member (the *de los dientes pa'fuera* false-leader case, inside "Identify, recruit, develop leadership"), A8.5 repair (inside "celebrate" and "keep the conflict on the issue"), A10.2/A10.4/A10.6 leader-self-care / collective-care / satisfaction-and-retention (inside "founder bottleneck", "burnout", and "task design"). **Reason: depth follows the source; Ganz is genuinely thin on these and they are better as inline links than padded entries.**
